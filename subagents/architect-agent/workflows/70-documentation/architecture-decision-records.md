# architecture-decision-records

**Group:** 70-documentation  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/solution-architect.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** sonnet

## Purpose

Build and maintain an immutable, driver-traced decision log: every architecturally-significant decision captured as a MADR ADR (context · decision drivers · ≥2 considered options · decision · consequences positive AND negative) so any newcomer can reconstruct the reasoning — not just what was decided but what was rejected and why. Answers: *is every significant decision recorded, traceable to a driver, and durable against the team that made it leaving?*

## Inputs & preconditions

- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the ADR-log location (`architecture/<slug>/adr/`), the existing stack + constraints, the compliance regime, team topology, and the decision-record conventions (MADR, zero-padded sequential, immutable once accepted).
- The architecture **drivers**: the prioritized quality-attribute scenarios from `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md` (the utility tree — each leaf a 6-part scenario ending in a response measure) and the constraints log from `workflows/00-drivers/constraints-assumptions-risks.md`. A decision that traces to no driver in the utility tree is accidental complexity — flag it, do not record it as a valid ADR; surface it to the controller to either tie to a driver or remove the decision.
- The **existing ADR corpus** (if any): the full set of `architecture/<slug>/adr/NNNN-*.md` files, read in sequence-number order, to establish which decisions are already accepted/superseded and which are open.
- The **decision candidates** for this run: either (a) a specific decision the controller has nominated (audit: does it have a complete ADR? design: draft the ADR) or (b) the full scope of a new design pass whose decisions need recording (design mode: the solution-architect authors one ADR per significant decision as the documentation step of the frame→explore→evaluate→document→verify loop — see `README.md`).
- Preconditions: `architecture/<slug>/adr/` directory exists (or is created); the utility tree (`quality-attribute-scenarios.md`) is present and current; if this flow runs as the document step inside the full orchestrator loop, the evaluator has already selected the winning direction and the writer has the tradeoff report in context.

## Oracle (source of truth)

- **hard (the `adrs_complete` oracle — deterministic, blocking):**
  - Every architecturally-significant decision has an ADR. "Significant" = a decision that meaningfully affects a quality-attribute scenario (performance, reliability, security, evolvability, cost) or that is a one-way door (expensive/impossible to reverse without a rewrite or a data migration). Decisions that affect no driver are not significant — do not paper over accidental complexity with an ADR.
  - Every ADR contains all mandatory sections: **context and problem statement** (the forces at play, the problem as a question), **decision drivers** (the specific QAS leaf(ves) from the utility tree — `QAS-*` refs + their response measures), **≥2 considered options** (a genuine alternative, not a strawman — an ADR with one option is not a decision), **decision outcome** (the chosen option + the reason it best satisfies the highest-priority drivers), and **consequences: positive AND negative** (what this buys AND what it costs on other attributes). An ADR missing any of these sections fails the gate.
  - Driver refs in every ADR trace to named scenarios in the utility tree (`QAS-AVAIL-02`, `QAS-PERF-01`, etc.) — not to prose goals. A `Driver refs` field that contains a vague goal ("performance") with no QAS pointer and no response measure fails the gate.
  - ADRs are **immutable once accepted** — status `proposed → accepted → deprecated/superseded by ADR-NNNN`. A previously accepted ADR that has been edited in place (not superseded) is a broken log; flag it as `critical`. A change to a prior decision is a new ADR that links both ways (the old ADR gains `superseded by ADR-NNNN`; the new ADR gains `supersedes ADR-NNNN`).
  - **One-way-door decisions are flagged** — the `One-way door? yes` field is present and answered. One-way doors (datastore choice, public API contract, auth model, prod-deploy topology, a schema that migrating away from requires downtime) must be explicitly called out so future maintainers know the reversal cost without re-analyzing the decision. A one-way door with `One-way door? no` or the field missing blocks.
  - **Sequence numbers are unique and sequential** — no gaps, no reused numbers, zero-padded. A reused or out-of-order number is a broken log.

- **soft (rubric, advisory — scored only after hard oracles are green):**
  - The ADR log captures the **durable why** — a newcomer reading the log can reconstruct why the team is where it is without talking to anyone who was there. The rejected options section is half the value: it stops the team re-litigating settled ground and surfacing "why didn't we just use X?" when X is already documented as rejected. An ADR that only names the winner without explaining what the runner-up offered (and what made it lose) scores low on this dimension.
  - The consequences section is **honest** — positive and negative consequences are proportionate to the real tradeoffs, not marketing copy for the chosen option. "No downsides" is a red flag; the evaluator penalizes it explicitly.
  - The corpus is **navigable** — the ADRs are sequenced logically, a supersede chain can be followed to the current accepted state, and the `README.md` at `architecture/<slug>/` surfaces the live decisions without requiring the reader to diff every ADR file.

## Standards & techniques

- **MADR (Markdown Architectural Decision Records)** — the template and lifecycle from `../shared/adr-template.md`. MADR is preferred over Nygard's original minimal format because it enforces the options + pros-and-cons structure that prevents the "one-option ADR" failure mode. The template is the normative form: `Status`, `Date`, `Deciders`, `Driver refs`, `One-way door?`, `Context and problem statement`, `Decision drivers`, `Considered options`, `Decision outcome` (with `Consequences` sub-sections: `Positive` + `Negative` + `Follow-ups / fitness functions`), `Pros and cons of the options`, `Links`.
- **Immutable-supersede lifecycle** — `proposed → accepted → deprecated / superseded by ADR-NNNN`. The lifecycle makes the log a **decision trail** (an immutable append-only record) rather than a wiki page. A future change opens a new ADR; the old one is marked `superseded by` and its `Links` section gains the forward pointer. This is the architectural analog of the "event sourcing" pattern applied to the decisions themselves: you can replay the log and arrive at the current architecture.
- **Decision-driver traceability to the utility tree** — every `Driver refs` entry must name a `QAS-*` scenario with its response measure. This is the mechanism by which the decision log stays honest: you cannot record a driver you can't point to, which means the utility tree must be current before ADRs are authored. Cross-reference `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md` to pull the live scenario IDs.
- **≥2 options + consequences requirement** — the minimum bar for a genuine architectural decision. Options should represent real design alternatives that the team actually considered (or that a reasonable architect would consider), not a strawman designed to lose. Each option carries its pros, cons, and tradeoff against the decision drivers — scored against the utility tree, not prose assertions.
- **One-way-door flagging** — per the reversibility dimension in `../shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` (Evolvability / Reversibility soft oracle) and ATAM's sensitivity-point analysis (`../shared/evaluation-method.md`). One-way-door decisions warrant deeper analysis (more options, more explicit consequences, explicit risk entries in the register) than reversible ones; the flag communicates that cost to future readers. Two-way-door decisions (easily reversed) are flagged `no` and analyzed proportionally — do not over-analyze a decision that can be cheaply undone.
- **ATAM sensitivity + tradeoff points as ADR substance** — when the ATAM evaluation (`workflows/60-evaluation/architecture-evaluation-atam.md`) identifies a sensitivity point (a decision that strongly determines whether a scenario is met) or a tradeoff point (a decision that pulls two scenarios in opposite directions), the ADR for that decision must name it explicitly in `Consequences.Negative` and in `Pros and cons of the options`. The CBAM analysis (`workflows/60-evaluation/tradeoff-analysis-cbam.md`) quantifying cost vs benefit belongs in the ADR's options comparison when cost is a driver.
- **Fitness functions as follow-ups** — the `Follow-ups / fitness functions` sub-section of `Consequences` names the automated checks that enforce the decision in CI. For example, an ADR choosing the modular-monolith boundary carries a fitness function: "no import from `claims` module into `auth` module (dependency-rule check)." These specs feed `workflows/60-evaluation/fitness-functions.md` and make the decision continuously verifiable, not just historically documented.

## Step sequence

- **audit:** Read the existing ADR corpus at `architecture/<slug>/adr/` in sequence-number order → for each ADR check: all mandatory sections present (context · ≥2 options · decision · consequences positive AND negative · driver refs); driver refs resolve to named `QAS-*` entries in `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`; status is valid (`proposed`/`accepted`/`deprecated`/`superseded by NNNN`/`rejected`); one-way-door field present and answered; no accepted ADR has been edited in place (compare content vs the status — an `accepted` ADR that has no `superseded by` pointer but whose context/options have changed is a flag) → cross-reference the full set of accepted ADRs against the utility tree to find orphan drivers (a `QAS-*` scenario with no ADR that decides how it is satisfied) → emit findings per `shared/finding-schema.md` grouped by `type:"documentation"` (missing ADR for a significant decision is `major`; missing consequences is `major`; edited-in-place accepted ADR is `critical`; missing one-way-door flag is `minor`; orphan driver is `major`). Read-only — authors nothing, edits no source.

- **design:** Frame (read `project-architecture-config.md` + the utility tree + the evaluator's chosen direction and tradeoff report — confirm the list of decisions that need ADRs; read existing ADR corpus to get the next sequence number) → Explore (for each decision to document, the solution-architect identifies ≥2 genuine options and maps each against the decision drivers — this is read-only analysis, not writing yet; any decision reducible to one option is escalated to the controller as "not a genuine decision, confirm or provide the alternative") → Evaluate (skeptical self-check: do the options genuinely differ? does each consequence entry name an attribute it affects, with a direction? are one-way doors flagged? are driver refs pointed at real QAS IDs?) → Document (ONE writer — the solution-architect — authors each ADR at `architecture/<slug>/adr/NNNN-<kebab-title>.md` per `../shared/adr-template.md`; assign sequence numbers sequentially from the last existing ADR; use `proposed` status until the controller accepts; cross-link supersede chains for any decision that revisits a prior ADR; write the fitness-function follow-ups in `Consequences`) → Verify (run the hard-oracle checklist: every new ADR has all mandatory sections; driver refs resolve in `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`; one-way doors flagged; sequence numbers are unique and sequential; no edit to a previously accepted ADR; update `architecture/<slug>/README.md`'s decision summary table) → loop ≤ 5 (per ADR, if a hard-oracle check fails, fix the specific gap and re-verify — do not re-explore; this is a documentation loop, not a design loop).

## Assertions & exit gate

- Every ADR in the corpus has status `proposed`, `accepted`, `rejected`, `deprecated`, or `superseded by ADR-NNNN` — no ADR with an absent or invalid status field.
- Every ADR has all six mandatory components: context and problem statement · decision drivers (with `QAS-*` refs + response measures) · ≥2 considered options (genuine alternatives) · decision outcome (chosen option + rationale against drivers) · consequences with both a `Positive` and a `Negative` sub-section · `Links` (supersede chain where applicable).
- Every `Driver refs` entry resolves to a named scenario in `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`; no prose-only driver reference.
- Every one-way-door decision has `One-way door? yes` and an explicit statement in `Consequences.Negative` of the reversal cost and the mechanism by which it becomes locked in (e.g. "once data is written in this schema, migration requires a multi-hour offline window").
- No accepted ADR has been edited in place — the only mutation on an `accepted` ADR is adding `superseded by ADR-NNNN` to its `Status` field and its `Links`.
- Every prioritized `(H,*)` quality-attribute scenario in the utility tree traces to ≥1 `accepted` or `proposed` ADR that decides how the system satisfies it. No orphan `(H,*)` driver.
- Sequence numbers are unique, sequential, and zero-padded; no reuse, no gaps within the authored set.
- The `architecture/<slug>/README.md` decision summary table is current — every accepted ADR appears in it with its one-line decision and its QAS refs.
- **Gate:** all hard-oracle checks above green AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 on the soft oracles (durable-why, honest consequences, navigable corpus).

## Output

Write `artifacts/architecture-decision-records/report.json` per `shared/report-format.md` — findings per `shared/finding-schema.md` (`type:"documentation"`; `oracle:"hard"` for missing mandatory section / unresolved driver ref / edited-in-place accepted ADR / missing one-way-door flag / orphan `(H,*)` driver; `oracle:"soft"` for shallow rejected-options section or one-sided consequences); the verification block (hard-oracle results + rubric score + the list of ADR IDs authored or audited). For design mode, the authored ADRs land at `architecture/<slug>/adr/NNNN-*.md` per `../shared/adr-template.md`; the updated decision summary table lands in `architecture/<slug>/README.md`. The run report (`report.json`) is the machine record; the ADRs in `architecture/<slug>/` are the durable architecture artifacts.

## Guardrails

Per `../shared/guardrails.md`: propose and document — never write product code. The solution-architect authors ADR markdown into `architecture/<slug>/adr/` (an architecture artifact); it never touches `src/**`, `frontend/**`, `backend/**`, or IaC that deploys real infra. The evaluator and audit path are read-only (no `Edit`/`Write`); only the solution-architect writes. Ground every ADR in a driver from the utility tree — a decision with no driver is accidental complexity; flag it to the controller rather than recording it. Record the tradeoff and the rejected alternatives — an ADR that documents only the winner is incomplete and fails the hard oracle. Never edit an accepted ADR in place; open a new superseding ADR. Never invent response measures or driver numbers not present in the BRD/PRD/`workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md` (`project-architecture-config.md` locked rule). Traceability completeness is verified by cross-referencing this flow's output against `workflows/70-documentation/traceability-mapping.md` — every ADR must appear in the driver → decision → component matrix there; an ADR absent from the traceability matrix is an orphan artifact.
