# lightweight-evaluation

**Group:** 60-evaluation  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/architecture-evaluator.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** sonnet

## Purpose
Run a **right-sized architecture evaluation** when full ATAM is overkill — selecting and executing the appropriate lightweight method (SEI Lightweight Architecture Evaluation, Mini-ATAM, ARID, PBAR, or TARA) calibrated to risk level and stakeholder availability. Answers the entry question the risk-driven gate poses: *which evaluation effort does this risk register demand, and does the chosen method still produce driver-traced findings and a risk register meeting the selected method's coverage bar?*

## Inputs & preconditions
- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the existing stack + deployment target, scale/SLO targets, compliance regime, team topology, constraints, and where the architecture description / ADR log / diagram source live (`architecture/<slug>/`).
- The **risk register** — `shared/risk-register.md` (likelihood × impact per item) plus the one-way-door / two-way-door classification of the decision under review. This is the gate input: without a risk register the method cannot be selected; run `../00-drivers/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` first.
- The **drivers** as a utility tree: prioritized 6-part quality-attribute scenarios with response measures (`../00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`). If the tree is absent or scenarios lack response measures, surface them as open driver questions — do not invent numbers. The chosen lightweight method still consumes the utility tree as its oracle; it is not excused from driver-traceability.
- The **decision table** from `../shared/evaluation-method.md` (the method-selection menu by phase × time × stakeholder availability): the selected method must match the risk register output against that table. A mismatch is a hard oracle failure — over-process or under-process alike.
- Target: the system/subsystem/design/as-built codebase under evaluation; the relevant `(H,*)` scenarios from the utility tree; the ADRs and architecture description in `architecture/<slug>/` (design mode) or scanned from the repo (audit mode/TARA).

## Oracle (source of truth)
The external truth is the **risk-driven method-selection gate** (`../shared/evaluation-method.md`) and the **utility tree** (the prioritized scenarios with response measures). Split:

- **hard:**
  - The method applied was selected by the risk-driven gate (risk register + the decision table in `../shared/evaluation-method.md`) — applying a method that does not match the risk×time×stakeholder cell is a hard-oracle failure for **under-process** (a single ADR against a one-way-door, high-risk decision skips the analysis the gate demands, leaving a scenario un-analyzed — see the driver-traced and met/unmet checks below, which *are* hard).
  - The chosen lightweight method produces **driver-traced findings**: every finding carries a `driver_ref` tying it to a named `QAS-*` scenario or constraint; a finding with no driver is dropped as a preference.
  - Every evaluated `(H,*)` scenario has an identified architectural approach whose response measure is judged met or unmet by a capacity model, a named pattern's guarantee, or a fitness function — never by assertion alone.
  - The output includes a **risk register** (likelihood × impact per risk, the threatened `QAS-*`, and a disposition: accept / mitigate / monitor) regardless of which lightweight method is applied.

- **soft:**
  - Evaluation effort is proportional to risk: `(H,H)` scenarios in the utility tree receive maximum scrutiny; `(L,L)` scenarios are treated as noise and get a pass/skip, not detailed analysis.
  - **Over-process flag** (per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`, the cited source of truth for this gate): applying full ATAM to a sub-6-hour, low-risk window is a rubric/craft judgement, not a deterministic gate failure — flag it `oracle:"soft"` and penalize at the rubric, same as the under-process case (a single ADR against a one-way-door, high-risk decision) that the same line names.
  - The lighter method still grounds findings in the utility tree — a lightweight report that drifts into general best-practice commentary without tracing each point to a scenario or constraint is penalized at the rubric.
  - Where two approaches both meet the drivers, the simpler one is preferred; accidental complexity is named and rejected with a note (penalize over-engineering per the rubric's length/novelty-bias guards).

## Standards & techniques
- **SEI Lightweight Architecture Evaluation** (Clements, Kazman, Klein — SEI/CMU) — the internal-team, time-boxed variant of ATAM; a few high-priority scenarios, no external stakeholder workshop required; the spine of this flow for `M/M` or lower risk.
- **Mini-ATAM** — a condensed ATAM pass (steps 4–9) executed by the internal team against the highest-priority `(H,*)` scenarios only; appropriate when a subset of the utility tree concentrates the risk; cross-ref `architecture-evaluation-atam.md` for the full 9-step method this condenses.
- **ARID (Active Review of Intermediate Designs)** (Clements & Northrop, SEI Technical Report CMU/SEI-2000-TR-004) — a structured peer-review of an incomplete or intermediate design, where the reviewer generates concrete use-cases and walks them through the partial design to find gaps; applied when the design exists but is not yet complete (EXPLORE phase); outputs a gap + open-question list, not a scored utility tree.
- **PBAR (Pattern-Based Architecture Review)** — evaluate the design's fitness by checking each applied pattern against its known contract, tradeoffs, and failure modes (the catalog in `../80-patterns/`); use when the design is a recognizable composition of named patterns and the risk concentrates in pattern selection and configuration, not novel structure.
- **TARA (Tiny Architecture Review of Implemented Systems)** — a metric + fitness-function scan of the real, running or committed codebase: dependency graphs, coupling/cohesion metrics, cycle detection, layering violations, and a targeted `(H,*)` scenario walk over the actual topology; use when evaluating *as-built* code, not a design artifact; writes no output back to the system.
- **Risk-driven method selection** (Fairbanks, *Just Enough Software Architecture*, ch. 5) — the entry gate: read the risk register + the decision table in `../shared/evaluation-method.md`; pick the method from the menu by *phase × time × stakeholder availability*; document the selection rationale in the report; never run more process than the risk demands.
- **6-part quality-attribute scenario** (SEI canonical form) — *source · stimulus · artifact · environment · response · response-measure* — the unit of analysis regardless of which lightweight method is used; a scenario without a measurable response measure is not a valid leaf.
- **ISO/IEC 25010** quality-attribute taxonomy — used to check that the utility tree covers the attributes that matter for this system; a lightweight evaluation does not exempt coverage from the taxonomy.
- **The canonical tradeoffs** (`../shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`) — CAP/PACELC, latency↔throughput, coupling↔autonomy, sync↔async, normalization↔read-performance, cost↔resilience; any lightweight evaluation that touches a cross-service boundary runs the 8-fallacies check on the `(H,*)` scenarios touching that boundary.

## Step sequence

- **audit:**
  1. **Risk gate** — read `../shared/risk-register.md`; classify each pending decision as one-way-door vs two-way-door; look up the risk×time×stakeholder cell in the `../shared/evaluation-method.md` decision table; select the method; record the selection rationale as the first output block. If the risk register is absent or empty, surface it as a blocking gap (cannot proceed; report `status: error`).
  2. **Driver confirmation** — read the utility tree from `architecture/<slug>/quality-attributes.md` (or restate available NFRs as 6-part scenarios and flag gaps); identify the `(H,*)` scenarios that are in scope for this evaluation window.
  3. **Method execution (read-only):**
     - *Lightweight / Mini-ATAM*: walk the architecture against each in-scope `(H,*)` scenario; identify the architectural approach; judge met/unmet by capacity model / named-pattern guarantee / fitness function; name sensitivity and tradeoff points; flag risks with likelihood × impact.
     - *ARID*: generate concrete use-cases from the in-scope scenarios; walk each through the intermediate design; record gaps, open questions, and missing decisions as findings.
     - *PBAR*: for each applied named pattern, check its known contract and failure modes from `../80-patterns/`; flag any mismatch against a `(H,*)` scenario response measure.
     - *TARA*: run structural checks on the as-built codebase (dependency graph, coupling metrics, cycle detection, layering); walk each `(H,*)` scenario over the actual topology; flag structural violations as hard findings.
  4. **Risk register** — cluster identified risks into themes tied to business drivers; record each with likelihood × impact, threatened `QAS-*`, and disposition (accept / mitigate / monitor). Non-risks (decisions confirmed safe given the drivers) are recorded alongside risks.
  5. **Over/under-process check** — confirm the method applied matches the risk-driven gate; under-process (a single ADR against a one-way-door, high-risk decision) is a hard finding once it surfaces as an un-analyzed `(H,*)` scenario or an un-traced risk; over-process (full ATAM on a sub-6h, low-risk window) is a soft finding per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`.
  6. Emit findings + risk register. Read-only: authors no artifact, edits no source.

- **design:**
  1. **Risk gate + driver confirmation** (same as audit steps 1–2, read-only).
  2. **Explore** — only when evaluating *competing* directions: score each option against every in-scope `(H,*)` scenario (does it meet the measure, at what tradeoff?); build a decision matrix; order-swap options to kill position bias per `../shared/evaluation-method.md` evaluator-discipline rules.
  3. **Evaluate** — execute the selected lightweight method (same logic as audit step 3); name sensitivity and tradeoff points; cluster risks into themes; confirm non-risks.
  4. **Document** — the **one writer** (`solution-architect`) folds the analysis into `architecture/<slug>/tradeoffs.md`: the method-selection rationale, the in-scope scenario×approach scorecard (met/unmet + the model that proves it), the sensitivity and tradeoff points, the risk register with themes, and the non-risks. Any decision the evaluation changes raises a new ADR in `architecture/<slug>/adr/` (`../shared/adr-template.md`). The evaluator never edits the architecture it grades.
  5. **Verify** — re-score the documented artifacts (not the prose): every in-scope `(H,*)` scenario has a met/unmet verdict proven by a model/pattern/fitness-function; every risk has a theme + likelihood × impact; every tradeoff point names both conflicting attributes; the method-selection rationale is present and matches the risk-driven gate. Hard-oracle failures loop back to step 3; soft failures raise advisory findings. Loop ≤ 5 iterations or pass; the loop may only re-close after an **external signal** (a passing executable fitness function or a human decision) — never on the evaluator's word alone past two internal iterations (anti reward-hacking per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`).

## Assertions & exit gate
- The method applied is the one the risk-driven gate selected (risk register + decision table in `../shared/evaluation-method.md`); under-process (a lighter method than the one-way-door/high-risk cell demands) is a hard-oracle failure once it manifests as an un-analyzed `(H,*)` scenario or an un-traced risk; over-process (full ATAM on a sub-6h, low-risk window) is a soft finding, per the same source's classification.
- Every in-scope `(H,*)` scenario has an identified architectural approach with a **met/unmet** verdict proven by a capacity model, a named pattern's guarantee, or a fitness function — no scenario left un-analyzed within the evaluation window, no verdict by assertion.
- The output includes a risk register: every risk carries likelihood × impact, the threatened `QAS-*` scenario, a risk theme tied to a business driver, and a disposition (accept / mitigate / monitor).
- Non-risks (decisions confirmed safe) are recorded alongside risks — a scorecard showing only downside is as incomplete as one showing only upside.
- A sub-6h, low-risk decision window that was evaluated under full ATAM instead of a lightweight method is flagged as an over-process finding (`oracle:"soft"`, `type:"risk"`, `severity:"minor"`) per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`'s soft-oracle classification of this check.
- No `(H,*)` scenario in the evaluation window relies on an approach that assumes a fallacy away on its critical path (`../shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`).
- Where two approaches both meet the drivers, the simpler one is preferred; the over-engineered alternative is named and rejected.
- **Gate:** hard oracles green (method-selection match on the under-process side, driver-traced findings, risk register present with themes, no asserted response-measure verdict, no fallacy violation on a `(H,*)` path) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 on the authored scorecard, which folds in the over-process soft flag.

## Output
Write `artifacts/lightweight-evaluation/report.json` per `shared/report-format.md` — the `drivers[]` block (the in-scope prioritized scenarios), `findings[]` per `finding-schema.md` (`type:"risk"` for register items with `severity = likelihood × impact`; `oracle:"hard"` for an under-process method-selection mismatch, an un-analyzed `(H,*)` scenario, an asserted response-measure verdict, or a risk with no theme; `oracle:"soft"` for an over-process mismatch — full ATAM on a sub-6h, low-risk window, per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`), and the verification block (method-selection rationale, per-scenario met/unmet + the model/pattern/fitness-function that proves it, the sensitivity points, the tradeoff points, the risk register with themes, the non-risks, and — for option comparison — the decision matrix + winner with grafted ideas). In design mode the authored scorecard lands in `architecture/<slug>/tradeoffs.md` and any decision the evaluation changes is recorded as an ADR in `architecture/<slug>/adr/`; the report lists those paths. Open driver questions (an unstated response measure that blocks a `(H,*)` verdict) are listed explicitly for the controller — never silently assumed. Cross-references: `../shared/evaluation-method.md` (the method-selection menu), `../shared/risk-register.md` (the risk register input), `60-evaluation/architecture-evaluation-atam.md` (the full ATAM flow this condenses for lower-risk windows).

## Guardrails
Per `shared/guardrails.md`: **propose & evaluate — never write product code and never edit the architecture being graded.** Ground every finding in a driver — a risk with no threatened `QAS-*` scenario is a preference, not a finding; drop it. Prove each response-measure verdict by a capacity model, a named pattern's guarantee, or a fitness function — never by assertion alone (`../shared/evaluation-method.md`). The method-selection is driven by the risk register + the decision table, never by convenience or habit; document the selection rationale in the report so it is reviewable. Run the 8-fallacies check on any `(H,*)` scenario path that crosses a service boundary (`../shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`). Penalize over-process: a lightweight method applied to a low-risk, reversible decision is the correct choice, not a shortcut — flag escalation to full ATAM on a low-risk window as a soft finding (`oracle:"soft"` per `../shared/evaluation-method.md`'s classification of this check), scored at the rubric alongside the under-process case. The evaluator never closes the revise loop on its own word past two iterations; an external signal (passing fitness function or human decision) is required to re-close (`../shared/evaluation-method.md` — external-signal gate on the revise loop). Never write to project memory — findings and lessons belong in the returned report and the authored ADRs; the controller curates memory.
