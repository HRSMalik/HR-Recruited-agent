# risk-storming-premortem

**Group:** 60-evaluation  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: subagents/architect-agent/agents/architecture-evaluator.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** opus

## Purpose

Surface architectural risks collaboratively by running **risk-storming** (independent risk identification over the architecture views, then convergence and ranking by likelihood × impact) followed by a **pre-mortem** ("it is one year from now and the system has failed — why?"), and turn all identified risks into a prioritized, fully-disposed risk register. Answers: *what can go wrong in this architecture, how bad would it be, and what are we doing about it?*

## Inputs & preconditions

- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the existing stack and deployment target (per `project-architecture-config.md` — e.g. a web SPA frontend, an API backend, a primary datastore, a reverse-proxy/gateway, and the team size), the compliance regime (per `project-architecture-config.md` — e.g. PII handling, RBAC tenant-scoping, audit logging), the phase scope (per `project-architecture-config.md` — which layers are in scope now vs. deferred to a later phase; keep any deferred integration seam visible, do not risk-hunt the deferred layer itself), and the artifact root (`architecture/<slug>/`).
- The prioritized quality-attribute scenarios (the utility tree from `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`) — every risk must threaten at least one named QAS-* scenario or constraint; a risk with no threatened driver is not a risk, it is a worry.
- The current constraints, assumptions, and RAID register from `workflows/00-drivers/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` — this flow **consumes** the RAID register's unvalidated assumptions and open sensitivity points and advances them to full risk dispositions; it does not re-derive the constraint set from scratch.
- The architecture views to storm over: at minimum the C4 L2 Container view (the deployable units and their relationships) and, where authored, the L3 Component view for the backend modules and the Deployment view (the project's runtime topology, per `project-architecture-config.md`). If views are not yet authored, the risk-storming step operates over a textual architecture description — note the degraded coverage in the report.
- The ATAM output from `workflows/60-evaluation/architecture-evaluation-atam.md` if already run — its sensitivity points and tradeoff points are the highest-signal starting positions for the risk hunt. If the ATAM run has not been completed, this flow runs standalone and the sensitivity/tradeoff column in the risk register is populated by the storming step itself.
- Preconditions: `project-architecture-config.md` present; ≥1 architecture view (diagram or textual description) available; the utility tree has ≥1 QAS-* scenario (even provisional) so risks can be anchored to drivers. If no utility tree exists, this flow returns `status:blocked` — every risk must threaten something named, and without the utility tree the dispositions are guesses.

## Oracle (source of truth)

The **risk register** is the external artifact of record. Every entry in it is the hard oracle — grounded in a driver, a likelihood × impact pair with rationale, and a disposition. What "good" looks like is not a feeling; it is a structurally complete register.

- **hard:**
  - Every identified risk carries: a unique `ARCH-RISK-NNN` id, a likelihood (H/M/L) and impact (H/M/L) each with a one-sentence rationale (not bare labels), a disposition (accept / mitigate / monitor / transfer — one of the four canonical options), the QAS-* id or constraint name it threatens, and (for `mitigate`) a concrete mitigation — either a pointer to an ADR-shaped decision that addresses it or an explicit statement of the architectural change required. A risk entry missing any of these fields fails the hard gate.
  - The pre-mortem covers **at least the four realistic failure-mode dimensions** for this system: (1) data integrity / consistency (the primary datastore's replication/failover posture, per `project-architecture-config.md`, and the core write paths through it), (2) security / breach (sensitive-data classes handled, the auth model and its secret-management posture, tenant/RBAC scoping — per `project-architecture-config.md`), (3) operational / team (team size and ownership boundaries, Conway's constraint, on-call/runbook maturity — per `project-architecture-config.md`), (4) evolvability / one-way-door (any deferred integration seam not yet cut, any provisional/mocked subsystem — a premature coupling here is irreversible). A pre-mortem that names only the obvious failure (e.g. "the database goes down") without the subtler failure modes in each dimension fails the hard gate.
  - Every (H,H) risk — likelihood High AND impact High — has either a concrete mitigation (an ADR-shaped structural change) or an explicit, controller-signed-off acceptance statement. A (H,H) risk disposed as "monitor" alone blocks; monitoring is not a mitigation for a critical risk.
  - One-way-door risks (decisions that, if wrong, require dismantling a hard-to-reverse design) are flagged `one_way_door: true` in the register. The datastore identity, the auth model, the prod-deploy topology, and any deferred integration seam are the known one-way-door candidate categories — each named in `project-architecture-config.md` must be explicitly risk-assessed, not silently assumed safe.
  - The risk hunt is **structurally divergent** before converging: it covers at minimum five domains — (ops/deployment), (security/trust), (data/consistency), (scale/performance), and (people/Conway's Law / team topology) — before the convergence step. A hunt that only finds database-down risks was not genuinely divergent.

- **soft:**
  - The risk hunt surfaces the **uncomfortable risks** — the ones the team is tempted to accept silently (e.g. "no replica for the primary datastore in the current design," "auth-secret rotation has no procedure," "the deferred integration seam is uncut and if the provisional subsystem stays mocked for N more months the schema will harden around it"). An honest register is more valuable than an optimistic one.
  - The pre-mortem imagines a **credible failure narrative**, not a checklist. The "why did it fail" must read as a plausible story (a sequence of events) — not isolated bullet points that could apply to any system.
  - Dispositions are **differentiated**: the register should not be all "mitigate" (wishful) or all "accept" (resigned). The mix of accept / mitigate / monitor reflects genuine triage, sized to the actual drivers (per `project-architecture-config.md` — do not right-size risks upward to justify over-engineering beyond the project's real scale and audience).
  - Risk themes are **named and clustered** per the ATAM method (`../shared/evaluation-method.md`): group individual risks into 3–5 systemic themes (e.g. "single points of failure in the data layer," "no coherent secret-management strategy," "team-topology risk under upcoming scope growth") and tie each theme to the business driver it threatens.

## Standards & techniques

- **Risk-storming** (independent-then-converge): each participant (or each agent persona in the evaluator's fan-out) independently annotates the architecture views — C4 L2, Deployment view, the trust-boundary diagram from `workflows/40-cross-cutting/security-architecture.md` if authored — marking components or relationships that concern them. Only after independent marking do the results converge: overlap indicates consensus risk; single-person findings get extra scrutiny (not dismissal). This ordering kills anchoring bias — the first person to speak does not set the frame for everyone else.
- **The pre-mortem** (Klein, 1989 / Kahneman): assume the system is already in production and has already failed in a significant way — a core business record is lost, data is breached, the system is unavailable for a full business day, a customer's PII is exposed. Work backwards: what sequence of architectural decisions and missing controls caused it? The pre-mortem is most useful for surfacing the risks the team is psychologically committed to dismissing (because they built the design). It runs *after* the risk-storming convergence to stress-test the register's completeness.
- **Risk matrix** (likelihood × impact, each H/M/L): a 3×3 grid with three zones — (H,H)/(H,M)/(M,H) = critical/major → require active disposition; (M,M)/(L,H)/(H,L) = minor → monitor or structured accept; (L,L)/(L,M)/(M,L) = cosmetic → accept with a log entry. Severity on the `finding-schema.md` object maps to risk level: (H,H) → `critical`, (H,M) or (M,H) → `major`, (M,M) → `minor`, (L,*) or (*,L) → `cosmetic`, **except** when the risk threatens a one-way-door decision — those floor at `major` regardless of likelihood.
- **Risk disposition** (the four canonical options): **accept** (log it, no structural change; rationale required — "acceptable because X"); **mitigate** (an architectural change or control reduces the likelihood or impact — must name the change); **monitor** (acceptable today at current scale but track a leading indicator — must name the metric and the threshold that triggers escalation to mitigate); **transfer** (insurance, SLA, contractual — rare for architectural risks, required to name the mechanism).
- **ATAM sensitivity and tradeoff points** (`../shared/evaluation-method.md`): a sensitivity point is a decision whose single value strongly determines whether a QAS is met (e.g. "single-primary datastore with no replica: if it goes down, writes fail — availability QAS is *sensitive* to this topology"); a tradeoff point is a decision that pulls two or more QAS scenarios in opposite directions (e.g. "a synchronous API between frontend and backend: a tradeoff point — adds latency to every user action but reduces eventual-consistency risk in the core write path"). Both feed directly into the risk register.
- **The 8 fallacies of distributed computing** (`../shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`): even a simple two-tier stack (frontend + backend + datastore) has cross-process calls (client → gateway/proxy → application server → datastore) — the fallacies apply at every hop. Check: is the network assumed reliable (no timeout on the client's request calls)?  Is latency assumed zero (no latency budget per screen)?  Is the network assumed secure (TLS/mTLS between the internal hops)?  Each fallacy assumed away becomes a risk entry.
- **Conway's Law** (`../shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`): the team topology (per `project-architecture-config.md` — team size, module/branch ownership) is an architectural constraint. Risks that arise from the team structure — a single owner as a SPOF for a critical module, a branch-divergence risk if a long-lived feature branch and the trunk drift, an onboarding risk for a scope expansion — are architectural risks, not just project risks.
- **One-way-door / reversibility** (`../shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md`, `../shared/adr-template.md`): irreversible decisions at risk of being wrong are flagged `one_way_door: true` and escalated to the controller before a design is authored around them. Do not paper over a one-way-door risk to keep the evaluation loop moving.
- **STRIDE** (for security-domain risks in the storming pass, anchored to trust boundaries from `workflows/40-cross-cutting/security-architecture.md`): each trust boundary gets a quick S/T/R/I/D/E scan during the storming step — not a full threat model (that is `security-architecture.md`'s job), but enough to ensure STRIDE-class risks surface in the register with a category tag so they can be traced to the full threat model later.

## Step sequence

- **audit:** Read `project-architecture-config.md` and the utility tree → consume the RAID register from `workflows/00-drivers/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` (read-only) → read the ATAM sensitivity/tradeoff points from `workflows/60-evaluation/architecture-evaluation-atam.md` if present → load all available architecture views (C4 L2, Deployment, Component if present) → run the **divergent risk hunt**: independently annotate across five domains (ops/deployment, security/trust, data/consistency, scale/performance, people/Conway) without cross-contamination → converge: group overlapping risks, retain lone findings for extra scrutiny → run the **pre-mortem** over the converged risk set: "the system has already failed — why?" — work backward through plausible failure narratives across the four mandatory dimensions (data integrity, security/breach, operational/team, evolvability/one-way-door) → check: is every (H,H) risk actively dispositioned? are all one-way-door candidates (per `project-architecture-config.md`) explicitly assessed? does the pre-mortem add any risks the storming missed? → emit findings per `finding-schema.md` (`type:"risk"`) grouped by theme then severity (critical → cosmetic), plus the risk register; read-only, no artifact authored.

- **design:** Frame (read `project-architecture-config.md`, the utility tree, and the RAID register; confirm scope and available views; read-only) → Explore (fan out the independent risk hunt across five domains; each domain produces its own risk list before convergence; the pre-mortem runs after convergence as a completeness stress-test; read-only) → Evaluate (skeptical evaluator applies the hard gate: every risk entry complete with likelihood × impact + rationale + disposition + threatened QAS; every (H,H) risk concretely dispositioned; one-way-door risks flagged; five-domain coverage confirmed; pre-mortem covers all four mandatory failure-mode dimensions; order-swapped to kill anchoring bias) → Document (one writer — `solution-architect` — authors `architecture/<slug>/risk-register.md`: the risk matrix header, the five-domain risk table with all required fields, the pre-mortem narrative, the 3–5 systemic risk themes with driver linkages, and the open mitigations backlog as forward pointers to ADRs-to-be-authored; also writes `artifacts/risk-storming-premortem/report.json` per `../shared/report-format.md`) → Verify (fitness-function checks: register completeness — every entry has all required fields; (H,H) disposition check — none dispositioned as "monitor" alone; one-way-door coverage — all known candidates named in `project-architecture-config.md` assessed; five-domain coverage — all five domains have ≥1 entry; pre-mortem four-dimension check — all four mandatory failure modes addressed; evaluator re-scores from the authored artifact; loop ≤ 8 or pass).

## Assertions & exit gate

- Every risk entry in the register carries: `ARCH-RISK-NNN` id, likelihood (H/M/L) + rationale, impact (H/M/L) + rationale, threatened QAS-* id or constraint name, disposition (accept/mitigate/monitor/transfer), and — for `mitigate` — a pointer to the ADR or structural change. No entry is missing any field.
- The five-domain divergent hunt is structurally confirmed: at least one risk entry per domain (ops/deployment, security/trust, data/consistency, scale/performance, people/Conway). A domain with zero entries must carry an explicit "no risks found in this domain — rationale" note, not a silent omission.
- The pre-mortem covers all four mandatory failure-mode dimensions: data integrity / consistency, security / breach, operational / team, evolvability / one-way-door. A dimension with no pre-mortem finding must carry an explicit "no additional risks surfaced beyond those from storming — rationale."
- Every (H,H) risk has a disposition of either `accept` (with a controller-signed-off rationale) or `mitigate` (with a named structural change or ADR pointer). A (H,H) risk dispositioned as `monitor` alone blocks.
- The one-way-door candidates named in `project-architecture-config.md` (typically: datastore identity, the auth model, the prod-deploy topology, and any deferred integration seam) each have an explicit risk entry with `one_way_door: true` — even if the assessed risk level is low, the flag must be present.
- The risk register includes 3–5 systemic risk themes, each naming the cluster of individual risks it groups and the business driver it threatens.
- **Gate:** hard oracles green (every risk entry complete; every (H,H) actively dispositioned; five-domain coverage confirmed; pre-mortem four-dimension coverage confirmed; one-way-door candidates flagged) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 (register is honest about uncomfortable risks, dispositions are genuinely differentiated, pre-mortem tells a credible failure narrative rather than isolated bullet points).

## Output

- Write `artifacts/risk-storming-premortem/report.json` per `../shared/report-format.md` — all findings per `finding-schema.md` (`type:"risk"`; `oracle:"hard"` for a risk entry missing required fields, a (H,H) risk with no active disposition, a one-way-door candidate with no explicit assessment, or a domain/pre-mortem dimension gap; `oracle:"soft"` for craft gaps — an overly optimistic register, undifferentiated dispositions, a pre-mortem that reads as isolated bullet points). The report includes: the risk register (all entries), the systemic risk themes block (3–5 themes, each with constituent risk ids and the business driver threatened), the pre-mortem narrative (the four-dimension failure story), and the verification block (domain-coverage check, (H,H) disposition check, one-way-door coverage check, pre-mortem dimension check, rubric score).
- In **design mode**, the one writer (`solution-architect`) also authors `architecture/<slug>/risk-register.md` with four sections: (1) **Risk Matrix Summary** — the 3×3 grid populated with ARCH-RISK-NNN ids in each cell; (2) **Risk Register Table** — one row per risk, all required fields, sorted critical → cosmetic; (3) **Pre-Mortem Narrative** — the four-dimension failure story in prose, with each failure mode linked back to the risk ids it surfaced or confirmed; (4) **Systemic Risk Themes** — the 3–5 named themes with constituent risk ids, the driver each threatens, and the open mitigations backlog (forward pointers to ADRs-to-be-authored or design changes required). The `architecture/<slug>/` structure follows `project-architecture-config.md`.
- Mitigations that require a structural design decision are recorded as ADR stubs (status: `proposed`, driver refs, the decision question) in `architecture/<slug>/adr/` — one stub per open mitigation — for the controller to authorize and the `solution-architect` to fully author in a subsequent pass. Risk-storming does not author the full ADR; it opens the stub so the decision is tracked and not lost.

## Guardrails

Per `../shared/guardrails.md`: this flow proposes risk dispositions and documents the register — it never writes product code, never applies mitigations, and never authors full ADRs in a single pass with no controller review. Dispositions (accept/mitigate/monitor/transfer) proposed here are **provisional** — the controller makes the final call on every (H,H) risk and every one-way-door risk before the downstream design flows proceed. The evaluator is a separate skeptical pass and never grades an artifact it co-drafted. Audit mode is fully read-only — no artifact is authored, no source is edited. The risk hunt is bounded (≤ 8 loop iterations in design mode) so the session terminates. Risks without a driver reference are not findings — tie every entry to a named QAS-* scenario or a named constraint, or drop it. Do not surface risks for the internals of any deferred subsystem named out-of-scope in `project-architecture-config.md` — risk-hunt the seam (the integration boundary), not the deferred system. Do not invent likelihood or impact numbers; if a value is genuinely unknown, surface it as an open question with a validation method rather than guessing H/M/L and presenting it as fact.
