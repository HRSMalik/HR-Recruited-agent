# genai-risk-assessment

**Group:** 90-specialized · **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/solution-architect.md · **Mode:** audit + design · **Default model:** sonnet

## Purpose
Assess a generative-AI system against the **NIST AI 600-1 (Generative AI Profile)** risk dimensions — confabulation, information integrity, information security, value-chain/data-provenance, harmful bias/homogenization, and dangerous/CBRN content — since ISO 25010 predates GenAI and its quality characteristics do not capture the failure modes native to foundation models. The governing question: *for every high-severity GenAI risk dimension the use case activates, is there a design control or a signed-off accepted risk, and is that control structurally verifiable — not just asserted?* This flow complements `ml-ai-system-architecture.md` (which owns the inference-path structure, RAG topology, and eval/guardrail loop) by evaluating the **risk profile** of what the system *does* with generation — who it may harm, what it may fabricate, and what it ingests from outside its trust boundary.

## Inputs & preconditions
- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the existing stack, the compliance regime (PII classification, GDPR/HIPAA/SOC2 applicability), the deployment target, the team topology, and the architecture-description / ADR log / diagram source locations.
- The **architecture drivers / utility tree** from `00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md` — the prioritized 6-part scenarios including any safety, correctness, or compliance scenario that touches GenAI output. If no GenAI-specific scenario exists (no confabulation-rate target, no harmful-output refusal-rate, no PII-egress threshold), surface them as **open driver questions** — do not invent thresholds.
- The **use-case classification** for the system: (a) *consequential-decision* (the GenAI output directly drives a downstream action about a person — claims triage, coverage eligibility, credit, hiring); (b) *human-in-the-loop advisory* (a human reviews before any action); (c) *low-stakes draft-assistant* (the output is edited by a domain expert before use). The classification gates the scope of the battery: a consequential-decision system gets the full 12-dimension sweep; a low-stakes draft-assistant gets the proportionate subset. State the classification as an assumption if not declared in the BRD/PRD; surface it for the controller to confirm — the entire risk scope depends on it.
- The **NIST AI 600-1 Generative AI Profile** (the 12 risk dimensions): confabulation (hallucination), harmful bias/homogenization, data privacy, information security, information integrity (disinformation/synthetic content), value-chain/data-provenance, dangerous/CBRN content, human-AI configuration, intellectual property, obscene/degrading/harmful content, non-textual harmful content, and sociotechnical factors.
- Cross-references to read before running: `../shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` (the AI-system hard oracles), `agentic-threat-model.md` (agent↔tool trust boundaries, prompt-injection, excessive agency), `rag-evaluation.md` (retrieval faithfulness and grounding — the main confabulation control), `../40-cross-cutting/privacy-compliance-architecture.md` (PII classification and residency — the data-privacy and information-security controls).
- Preconditions: the system description (what the GenAI component generates, for whom, in what context, with what downstream effect), the provider/model identity (managed API vs self-hosted), the data provenance of any retrieval corpus or fine-tuning dataset, and whether the system makes decisions about people (the consequential-decision classification). A flow with no use-case classification and no GenAI-specific driver **downgrades to a scoping audit** and surfaces those gaps before proceeding.

## Oracle (source of truth)
The **NIST AI RMF** (AI Risk Management Framework) GOVERN/MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE functions applied via the **NIST AI 600-1 Generative AI Profile** risk dimensions — the canonical external standard for GenAI-specific risk, complementing ISO 25010 (which predates foundation models). Split:

**hard:** (deterministic — each is traceable, measurable, or structurally checkable; any failure blocks)
- **Confabulation control.** The system's high-severity confabulation path (a fabricated fact that drives a consequential downstream action — a claim amount, a coverage decision, a legal citation) has a design control whose grounding rate is **measurable**: a faithfulness/groundedness metric on a labeled set (e.g. faithfulness ≥ θ on the evaluation harness from `rag-evaluation.md`) **and** an output-side guardrail that validates grounding before the answer is surfaced or acted upon. "We use RAG" is not a control — a RAG pipeline with no measured faithfulness target and no grounding guardrail does not meet this oracle. If no faithfulness threshold is stated in the BRD/PRD, it is an open driver question, not a guessed number. Cross-ref `rag-evaluation.md` and `../shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` → *RAG grounded*.
- **Information Security on the model boundary.** The provider boundary is a **trust boundary**: PII and sensitive data sent to the model are **classified, minimized/redacted** before egress per the compliance regime, prompt templates and system-prompt contents are treated as confidential (not reconstructible from outputs), and **prompt-injection / indirect prompt-injection** (malicious content in the retrieval corpus or user input that hijacks the model's instructions) is threat-modeled with a structural control (input validation, instruction-hierarchy enforcement, or sandboxed tool execution). Every STRIDE category on the model trust boundary has a control or an accepted risk. An unredacted-PII egress to an external provider under a regulated regime is a **blocking hard finding**. Cross-ref `agentic-threat-model.md` and `../40-cross-cutting/security-architecture.md`.
- **Information Integrity (synthetic / disinformation content).** Where the system generates content that will be published, forwarded, or presented as factual to a third party (a claimant letter, a regulatory filing, a case-summary), there is a **human-review gate or a structural authenticity control** (provenance watermark, disclosure label, or a factual-grounding check) before the content is transmitted. A system that can emit fabricated regulatory statements or synthetic claimant communications with no review gate is a blocking hard finding.
- **Value-Chain / Data-Provenance.** The provenance of every data source that enters the model context (the retrieval corpus, any fine-tuning dataset, third-party embeddings or model weights) is **documented**: origin, licensing terms, PII classification, and any known contamination / bias flags. A retrieval corpus fed to the model whose license or PII status is undocumented, or a fine-tuning dataset with unknown provenance, is a **hard finding** — the model's outputs inherit the provenance risks of its inputs, and under GDPR/HIPAA an undocumented PII corpus creates a compliance gap even if PII is redacted at query time. Cross-ref `../40-cross-cutting/privacy-compliance-architecture.md` → data governance + lineage.
- **Bias / Homogenization on consequential-decision paths.** Where the system makes or materially influences a consequential decision **about a person** (claims triage ranking, coverage eligibility, fraud scoring, adjuster assignment), the assessment must document: (a) which protected attributes are present or proxied in the input/output space; (b) whether a fairness evaluation (e.g. demographic-parity gap, equalized-odds gap) has been run and what the measured disparity is; (c) whether the output population shows homogenization (the same answer regardless of input variation) that could mask individual circumstances. The **absence of a bias/fairness evaluation on a consequential-decision path is itself a blocking hard finding** — not a soft concern — because it is an un-accepted risk against a human-rights/compliance driver. Where the system is not on a consequential-decision path, bias is a soft dimension.
- **Dangerous / CBRN content.** Where the use-case domain plausibly surfaces queries or documents touching hazardous materials, weapons, or self-harm (an industrial-claims context with chemical-exposure claims; a workers-comp context with pharmaceutical overdoses), there is a **content classification step or a hard refusal policy** before the model generates detailed instructions or synthesis routes. If the domain analysis finds zero plausible surface area for CBRN content, this oracle is marked "not applicable" with a documented rationale — the assessment never silently skips it.

**soft:** (rubric-scored by `architecture-evaluator` after hard oracles green; mean ≥ 0.8)
- **Assessment proportionality.** The scope of the risk battery is proportionate to the use case: a consequential-decision system receives the full 12-dimension AI 600-1 sweep with quantitative targets; a low-stakes draft-assistant receives the five high-severity dimensions (confabulation, information security, information integrity, value-chain/data-provenance, and bias proportionate to its exposure). Over-assessing a low-stakes system is waste; under-assessing a consequential one is negligent — flag either direction.
- **Controls are designed in, not bolted on.** Risk controls (the grounding guardrail, the PII-redaction layer, the bias evaluation, the provenance registry) are named components in the architecture — with a described mechanism and a stated failure mode — not bullets in a risk register with no structural home. A list of controls with no architectural locus is a soft finding.
- **GOVERN function coverage.** The NIST AI RMF GOVERN function (policies, roles, accountability) is represented: there is a named **AI risk owner** for the system, a stated review cadence (e.g. post-model-update, post-data-refresh), and a mechanism for stakeholders (including affected parties in a consequential-decision context) to flag harms. The absence of any governance structure is advisory — it is a soft finding, not a gate — unless the compliance regime (GDPR, EU AI Act, state insurance regulation) mandates one, in which case it elevates to hard.

## Standards & techniques
- **NIST AI RMF (AI Risk Management Framework)** — the four functions: **GOVERN** (policies, accountability, roles, review cadences for AI risk), **MAP** (categorize the system, identify risk dimensions relevant to the use case, classify impact/likelihood), **MEASURE** (quantify each dimension: groundedness rate, bias-disparity metric, PII-egress count, provenance coverage), **MANAGE** (design controls, accepted risks, monitoring, incident response). This flow executes MAP→MEASURE→MANAGE per dimension; GOVERN is assessed as coverage evidence.
- **NIST AI 600-1 (Generative AI Profile)** — the 12 unique risk dimensions for GenAI systems (not present in the AI RMF base): confabulation (hallucination of facts, citations, quantities); harmful bias/homogenization (demographic disparity; loss of individual-level response variation); data privacy (PII in training/retrieval corpus, reconstruction attacks, membership inference); information security (prompt injection, model extraction, adversarial inputs); information integrity (synthetic disinformation, no-provenance outputs); value-chain/data-provenance (third-party model weights/data with unknown licensing, bias, or contamination); dangerous/CBRN content (chemical/biological/radiological/nuclear synthesis information); human-AI configuration (over-reliance, automation bias — humans deferring to a model past safe bounds); intellectual property (copyright/trade-secret leakage from memorized training data); obscene/degrading/harmful content; non-textual harmful content (synthetic audio/image/video); and sociotechnical factors (systemic impacts on labor, information ecosystems).
- **NIST AI RMF MAP function** — use-case categorization and risk-surface identification before scoring: classify the system on the **consequential-decision axis** (does output directly drive a decision about a person?), the **autonomy axis** (human-in-the-loop vs human-on-the-loop vs fully automated), the **data-sensitivity axis** (regulated PII present?), and the **domain-specific-harm axis** (CBRN surface? financial/legal decisions? health decisions?). The MAP step produces the **activated dimensions** — those to assess deeply — and the **not-applicable dimensions** with rationale.
- **Confabulation taxonomy and mitigation ladder** (from the RAG / LLM-reliability literature): closed-domain grounding (retrieval-augmented generation with a measured faithfulness metric) → attribution / citation display to the end user → output-side schema/fact validation → human-review gate as a fallback for low-confidence outputs. The **faithfulness@k / RAGAS faithfulness / FactScore** family are the measurable metrics; a confabulation control that cannot cite a metric is structural but unverified.
- **STRIDE applied to the model trust boundary** (from `../40-cross-cutting/security-architecture.md`): Spoofing (is the model identity verified — no model-substitution attack?), Tampering (is the prompt/context integrity-protected from indirect injection?), Repudiation (are prompt/response audit logs retained?), Information Disclosure (PII reconstruction from outputs?), Denial of Service (prompt-bomb / excessive-context attacks that exhaust context window or token budget?), Elevation of Privilege (jailbreak → tool call with excessive scope?). Cross-ref `agentic-threat-model.md` for the agent-specific variants (tool-poisoning, excessive agency, prompt injection via retrieved documents).
- **Fairness metrics for consequential decisions** — demographic parity gap (DP gap: difference in positive-output rate across demographic groups), equalized-odds gap (EOp gap: difference in true-positive and false-positive rates), and individual fairness (similar inputs → similar outputs). The metric choice depends on the decision type and the applicable legal regime (disparate-impact doctrine under US insurance regulation; Article 22 GDPR for automated decisions). A fairness evaluation that picks a metric without justifying it against the legal/ethical regime of the use case is incomplete.
- **Data provenance and supply-chain integrity** — for retrieval corpora: document origin (source URL / dataset DOI), license (CC-BY/commercial/proprietary), PII presence and classification result, known-bias flags from the dataset card; for fine-tuning datasets: SLSA provenance level, a signed SBOM of the training data, and membership-inference-risk assessment. The **value-chain risk** of a managed model API is that the provider's pre-training corpus is unknown — the design states this as an accepted risk with its scope (e.g. "the provider's training corpus is a black box; we mitigate confabulation risk via RAG grounding; we accept residual memorization/IP-leakage risk for public-domain content only"). Cross-ref `../50-infrastructure/cicd-architecture.md` → supply-chain-contract.md (SLSA).
- **Proportionality calibration** (Fairbanks, *Just Enough Software Architecture* applied to AI risk): the depth of assessment scales with use-case impact. Use the **consequential-decision / autonomy / data-sensitivity / domain** axes from the MAP function to pick: (a) *full battery* — all 12 AI 600-1 dimensions, quantitative targets, fairness evaluation, GOVERN coverage check; (b) *proportionate subset* — the five high-severity hard oracles above plus any domain-specific dimension the MAP function activates; (c) *scoping audit* — a MAP-only run that classifies the system, identifies the activated dimensions, and documents what a full assessment would need. A consequential-decision system that receives only the subset is a hard finding; a draft-assistant that receives the full battery is a soft finding (over-process).

## Step sequence
- **audit:** **MAP** the system — classify use case (consequential-decision / advisory / draft-assistant), autonomy level, data sensitivity, and domain harm surface → identify the **activated AI 600-1 dimensions** (the dimensions to assess deeply) and the not-applicable dimensions with rationale → **MEASURE** each activated dimension against its hard oracle: walk the existing/proposed architecture for the confabulation control (is there a faithfulness metric and an output guardrail? what is the measured rate?); walk the model trust boundary for the STRIDE controls and PII-egress path; examine information-integrity controls (is there a review gate for externally published generated content?); trace the retrieval corpus and any fine-tuning data to a provenance record (or flag the gap); assess bias/fairness if the system is on a consequential-decision path (does a fairness evaluation exist? what is the measured disparity?); check CBRN surface and refusal policy; assess GOVERN coverage (is there an AI risk owner, a review cadence, a harm-reporting path?) → **emit findings + risk register** (per `finding-schema.md`), read-only — author nothing, edit no source.
- **design:** **Frame** (confirm use-case classification, the activated dimensions, and the GenAI-specific quality-attribute scenarios from the utility tree; surface any dimension where no measurable target exists as an open driver question — do not proceed with invented thresholds; read-only) → **Explore** (≥2 genuinely divergent control-design directions for the highest-severity activated dimensions — e.g. for confabulation: *closed-domain RAG with a measured faithfulness guardrail* vs *human-in-the-loop verification for all generated outputs*; for information security: *PII-redaction-at-ingestion with a classification model* vs *on-the-fly redaction at prompt-assembly time*; for consequential-decision bias: *pre-deployment fairness evaluation on a held-out labeled set + demographic-parity threshold* vs *human-review gate for all consequential outputs*; each direction as a concrete spec with its structural mechanism, measurable target, operational cost, and failure mode; read-only) → **Evaluate** (the skeptical `architecture-evaluator` scores each direction per `evaluation-method.md` against the utility tree and the hard oracles; names sensitivity points — e.g. *confabulation rate is sensitive to retrieval precision, not just recall*; *PII-redaction accuracy is sensitive to the entity-recognition model's recall on domain-specific terms*; *fairness gap is sensitive to the demographic distribution of the evaluation set* — and tradeoff points — *closed-domain RAG reduces confabulation but adds retrieval-latency and index-maintenance cost; human-review gates reduce all GenAI risks but increase latency and operational cost; PII-redaction at ingestion is stronger but requires re-indexing on policy change vs on-the-fly which is weaker but more flexible*; order-swapped to kill bias) → **Document** (ONE writer authors the artifacts: the **GenAI risk register** (the activated-dimension × control matrix — dimension, severity, design control, measurable target, fallback, accepted-risk if no control is cost-justified), the **ADRs** for each one-way-ish control decision (confabulation-control strategy, PII-boundary mechanism, bias-evaluation policy, information-integrity gate, provenance-registry approach) with tradeoff + rejected alternatives, and an **update to the threat model** per `agentic-threat-model.md` for any new trust-boundary controls introduced; reuses established patterns from `rag-evaluation.md`, `agentic-threat-model.md`, and `../40-cross-cutting/privacy-compliance-architecture.md`; preserves conceptual integrity of the model-dependency strategy from `ml-ai-system-architecture.md`) → **Verify** (each hard oracle is checked against the authored artifacts: does the confabulation control name a measurable faithfulness target? is the PII-egress path closed? does the bias assessment cite a fairness metric and a measured disparity? is data provenance documented per activated source? is the CBRN surface addressed or documented as not-applicable? do the GOVERN elements — risk owner, review cadence, harm-reporting — appear? ADRs complete and diagrams compile? evaluator re-scores from artifacts) → loop ≤ ~10 or pass.

## Assertions & exit gate
- Every **activated AI 600-1 dimension** (per the MAP classification) has a design control or a documented, accepted risk — no dimension is silently un-addressed.
- **Confabulation** on any high-severity output path (one that drives a consequential downstream action or is externally published as factual) has a grounding control with a **named, measurable faithfulness target** (a metric + threshold from the eval harness in `rag-evaluation.md`) and an output-side guardrail before the output is surfaced. "We use RAG" with no metric does not meet this oracle.
- **Information Security** on the model trust boundary: PII classified + minimized/redacted before egress, STRIDE per trust boundary covered or accepted-risk documented, prompt-injection threat-modeled with a structural control (per `agentic-threat-model.md`), provider secret externalized (never in code, prompt, or diagram). An unredacted-PII egress under a regulated regime blocks.
- **Information Integrity**: any generated content that will be transmitted externally or presented as factual to a third party has a human-review gate or a provenance/disclosure control. A system that can autonomously emit externally-transmitted synthetic factual content with no gate is a blocking finding.
- **Value-Chain / Data-Provenance**: every data source in the model context (retrieval corpus, fine-tuning dataset, third-party embeddings) has a documented provenance record (origin, license, PII classification, known-bias flags). An undocumented corpus that feeds the model is a hard finding regardless of runtime redaction.
- **Bias / Homogenization**: if the system is on a **consequential-decision path**, a fairness evaluation exists with a named metric (demographic-parity gap, equalized-odds gap, or another metric justified against the legal/ethical regime), a measured disparity value, and either an accepted threshold or a remediation ADR. The absence of any fairness evaluation on a consequential-decision path blocks.
- **Dangerous / CBRN content**: the assessment documents whether the domain activates CBRN surface area; if yes, a content-classification step or hard refusal policy exists; if no, the not-applicable rationale is documented. Silent omission of this dimension blocks.
- **GOVERN coverage** (soft unless mandated): a named AI risk owner, a review-cadence statement, and a harm-reporting path are either present or documented as a gap with an open driver question.
- (design) **ADRs complete and diagrams compile**: every control-design decision (confabulation strategy, PII-boundary mechanism, bias-evaluation policy, information-integrity gate, provenance approach) has a MADR ADR with context · decision drivers · ≥2 considered options · decision · consequences (positive AND negative). Every diagram is diagrams-as-code that renders without error per `shared/diagramming-standards.md`.
- **Gate:** hard oracles green (confabulation control with named measurable target · PII-egress closed and STRIDE covered · information-integrity gate for externally-transmitted content · data provenance documented for all model-context sources · bias/fairness evaluation present on consequential-decision paths · CBRN surface addressed or not-applicable documented · all activated AI 600-1 dimensions have a control or accepted-risk · diagrams compile · ADRs complete) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 (assessment proportionate to impact · controls architecturally located · GOVERN coverage).

## Output
Write `artifacts/genai-risk-assessment/report.json` per `shared/report-format.md` — the `drivers[]` block (the GenAI-specific quality-attribute scenarios with response measures, or the open driver questions where a target is unstated), the **use-case classification** result (consequential-decision / advisory / draft-assistant; autonomy level; data-sensitivity; domain-harm surface), the **activated-dimension map** (which of the 12 AI 600-1 dimensions are in scope and why; which are not-applicable with rationale), `findings[]` per `finding-schema.md` using the full type enum (`security` for model-boundary STRIDE gaps and prompt-injection; `privacy` for PII-egress and data-provenance gaps; `compliance` for regulatory/fairness violations; `data` for provenance and corpus-documentation gaps; `observability` for missing faithfulness metrics or bias-disparity monitoring; `risk` for accepted-risk SPOFs and un-evaluated fairness exposures; `documentation` for missing ADRs or incomplete threat-model coverage; `operability` for absent GOVERN elements — risk owner, review cadence, harm-reporting), with `oracle:"hard"` for any blocking oracle failure (un-addressed high-severity dimension, no faithfulness metric on a confabulation path, unredacted-PII egress, no fairness evaluation on a consequential-decision path, undocumented corpus provenance, silent CBRN omission) and `oracle:"soft"` for craft gaps (controls not architecturally located, over/under-proportionate battery), and for design mode the `verification` block (faithfulness-target coverage, STRIDE coverage on the model trust boundary, data-provenance registry completeness, fairness-evaluation coverage, CBRN-surface disposition, GOVERN-coverage result, diagram-compile result, ADR-completeness result, rubric score). Authored artifacts land in `architecture/<slug>/` (e.g. `architecture/genai-risk/`) — the **GenAI risk register** (activated-dimension × control matrix), the **ADRs** (confabulation-control strategy, PII-boundary mechanism, bias-evaluation policy, information-integrity gate, provenance-registry approach), and the updated threat-model cross-referencing `agentic-threat-model.md`. Open driver questions (unstated faithfulness threshold, no fairness-metric choice, no bias-disparity target) are listed explicitly for the controller — never silently assumed. Cross-reference `../shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` (the AI-system hard oracles that this flow populates — the *GenAI risk addressed* oracle gate), `agentic-threat-model.md` (the agent↔tool trust-boundary threat model this flow extends on the model-boundary dimension), `rag-evaluation.md` (the faithfulness / grounding / retrieval-quality targets this flow cites as the measurable confabulation control), and `../40-cross-cutting/privacy-compliance-architecture.md` (the PII classification and data-residency controls this flow checks on the value-chain/data-provenance and information-security dimensions).

## Guardrails
Per `shared/guardrails.md`: **propose & document — never write product code, never call a live model, never apply infra, never write to project memory.** This flow authors the GenAI risk *artifacts* (the risk register, the ADRs, the threat-model update) into `architecture/<slug>/` — the controller implements the controls and the operator applies any infrastructure change. Ground every risk finding in a driver — a finding with no quality-attribute scenario or compliance requirement it serves is a preference, not a finding (`finding-schema.md`); either tie it to a stated driver or drop it. Never invent a faithfulness threshold, a bias-disparity target, or a CBRN refusal rate — if the BRD/PRD does not state the number, it is an open driver question for the controller to resolve. Reuse the established controls from `rag-evaluation.md`, `agentic-threat-model.md`, and `privacy-compliance-architecture.md` before inventing new mechanisms. One writer preserves conceptual integrity of the control strategy — controls for the same trust boundary must be coherent, not assembled from ad-hoc per-dimension patches. Record the tradeoff and the rejected alternatives on every control-design ADR — a grounding guardrail that doesn't state what it costs (latency, operational complexity, recall loss on valid outputs) and what it beat is an incomplete ADR. The MAP step (use-case classification) gates the scope: do not run the full 12-dimension battery on a low-stakes draft-assistant and do not run only the proportionate subset on a consequential-decision system — proportionality is itself an oracle, not a courtesy.
