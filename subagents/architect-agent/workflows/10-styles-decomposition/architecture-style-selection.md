# architecture-style-selection

**Group:** 10-styles-decomposition  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/solution-architect.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** opus

## Purpose
Choose the **macro architecture style** — monolith / modular-monolith / microservices / event-driven / serverless / SOA / space-based — by the prioritized drivers, not by fashion. This is the single highest-leverage, hardest-to-reverse structural decision in the system: it sets the deployment unit, the consistency boundary, the failure model, the team-ownership seams, and the operational surface area every later flow inherits. Answers one question: *given this utility tree and these constraints, what is the simplest style that meets the (H,*) scenarios, and what does it cost on the ones it doesn't serve best?* A wrong style here is a rewrite, not a refactor — so the bar is a one-way-door ADR with ≥2 styles compared, never a default reached for out of habit.

## Inputs & preconditions
- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the **existing shape** (e.g. an existing modular-monolith pair — a frontend SPA + an API service over a single primary datastore) and the stack lock; the deployment target; the team topology (the Conway input — who owns which branch/module); the scale posture (right-size to the project's actual scale, per config); the "locked rules" (boring-tech + existing-stack-first; do NOT propose microservices/a broker/multi-region/k8s without a concrete driver).
- The **drivers** — the utility tree from `../00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`: the prioritized 6-part scenarios with response measures (e.g. `QAS-PERF-*` a response-time budget; `QAS-SEC-*` RBAC scoping + audit; `QAS-EVOLVE-*` a deferred integration seam) and their `(business value, technical risk)` ranks. **No utility tree → no style selection:** you cannot pick a style without the attributes it is optimizing for. Downgrade scope to `status:error` and say so.
- Preconditions: the source-of-truth docs (BRD / PRD / understanding log) named in `project-architecture-config.md`, and any prior ADRs read; the `(H,H)` and `(H,M)` scenarios identified as the decision focus; the **team-scale and independent-deploy facts confirmed** (the "you must be this tall" inputs) — if unstated, surface as an open driver question, don't assume a service split is warranted.

## Oracle (source of truth)
The prioritized utility tree (`quality-attribute-scenarios.md`) + the style catalog's known per-attribute strengths/weaknesses + the canonical tradeoffs (`shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`) + the constraints in `project-architecture-config.md`. The chosen style is judged against *which (H,*) scenarios it serves and at what tradeoff*, never against how modern it looks.

- **hard:**
  - **Driver-justified, ≥2 styles compared.** The chosen style is argued against the **prioritized quality-attribute scenarios** in a **decision matrix** — at least two styles scored row-by-row (scenarios weighted by priority × candidate styles), showing *where each wins and loses*, not a single blended number that hides the tradeoff (per `shared/evaluation-method.md` "choosing between options"). A choice with only one option evaluated is not a decision — it blocks.
  - **One-way-door ADR.** The selection is recorded as a complete MADR ADR (`shared/adr-template.md`) with **`One-way door? yes`**, context · ≥2 considered options · decision · consequences (positive AND negative) · driver refs tracing to real `QAS-*` leaves. Missing alternatives, missing negative consequences, or an untraced driver fails `adrs_complete`.
  - **Accidental-complexity check (the cardinal hard gate here).** No distributed/decoupled style (microservices, event-driven, serverless, space-based, SOA) is selected **without a named driver that demands it** — an independent-deploy/team-scale/fault-isolation/elastic-scale scenario in the (H,*) band. A style whose benefits no scenario buys is accidental complexity and blocks (`shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` → *Driver traceability*: "a choice no driver demands"). The "you must be this tall to use microservices" bar — multiple teams needing independent deploy cadence, a real Conway split, a scale/isolation scenario the monolith can't meet — must be met *with cited scenarios* before a service split passes.
  - **Style-fallacy honesty.** Any distributed candidate accounts for the 8 fallacies where they now apply (network unreliable, latency non-zero, partial failure normal — `shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md` §1–8); a candidate that assumes them away (synchronous service chains with no timeout/fallback story, a "distributed monolith" that must deploy together) is disqualified or flagged, not scored as a peer.
- **soft:** **Right-sized** — the *simplest* style that meets the drivers wins (penalize the more elaborate option, per the rubric's length/novelty-bias guard); a CRUD-scale internal tool gets the modular monolith, not a service mesh. **Tradeoff honesty** — the analysis names what each *rejected* style was genuinely better at (the choice's blind spots), and the chosen style's seams for the *likely next change* (e.g. the near-term bounded contexts identified in the utility tree + any deferred integration seam named in `project-architecture-config.md`) are deliberate, not accidental.

## Standards & techniques
- **The architecture-style catalog** (Richards & Ford, *Fundamentals of Software Architecture*) — each style carried with its characteristic per-quality-attribute profile, so the decision matrix scores from a known baseline, not a guess:
  - **Monolith** — simplest deploy/test/operate, strong transactional consistency, lowest latency (in-process); weak on independent deploy, fault isolation, and selective scale. The default until a driver hurts.
  - **Modular monolith** — one deploy unit, clean module boundaries (enforced by a dependency fitness function), in-process calls; keeps the monolith's simplicity + ACID while buying evolvability and a clean seam to *later* extract a context. **The project default unless the config says otherwise** (`project-architecture-config.md`): right for most internal/enterprise tools on a small team.
  - **Microservices** — independent deploy/scale/team-ownership and fault isolation; pays with network latency, eventual consistency, distributed transactions (saga/outbox), and large operational surface. Demands the "you must be this tall" bar.
  - **Event-driven** — high decoupling, elastic throughput, async resilience; pays with eventual consistency, implicit end-to-end flow (choreography), and harder debugging/tracing.
  - **Serverless / FaaS** — elastic, pay-per-use, ops-offloaded; pays with cold-starts (latency), vendor lock-in, and statelessness constraints.
  - **SOA** — enterprise reuse via shared services + an ESB; pays with central-bus coupling and governance weight.
  - **Space-based** — extreme elastic scale by removing the central DB bottleneck (in-memory data grid); pays with consistency/complexity — reserved for genuine high-volume variable load.
- **Modular-monolith-first default** (Newman, *Monolith to Microservices*; Fowler, "MonolithFirst"): start with the well-factored modular monolith and extract services only when a driver forces it. Gall's Law (`shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`) — a working complex system evolves from a working simple one; you can't design the distributed system correct up front.
- **"You must be this tall to use microservices"** (Newman) — the prerequisites a service split *requires*: independent deployability (separate teams shipping on separate cadences), a real **Conway's-law** team split owning the boundaries, observability/automation maturity to operate N services, and a scenario the monolith genuinely can't meet (fault isolation, selective scale). Absent these, the split is cost with no driver.
- **One-way vs two-way-door analysis** (Bezos): the style is a **one-way door** (expensive to reverse) — so it earns deep analysis (≥2 options, a decision matrix, an ADR) and is flagged as such; two-way-door decisions downstream are made cheaply and not over-analyzed. Cross-ref `../60-evaluation/tradeoff-analysis-cbam.md` for the CBAM cost/benefit/ROI weighting when two styles both meet the drivers but differ in cost.
- **Decision matrix + position-bias kill** (`shared/evaluation-method.md`): score each style against every (H,*) scenario, swap the option order and re-score, ignore length/sophistication — the simpler style that meets every driver scores *higher*.
- **AKF scale cube** as the right-sizing lens: most scale needs are met by X-axis (horizontal clones of a stateless monolith) + Y-axis is the functional split a service decomposition adds — only reach for Y/Z when X can't meet the scale scenario. Confirms whether scale is even a style driver here.

## Step sequence
- **audit:** Frame the (H,*) drivers from the utility tree → name the **current/proposed style** and walk it against each high-priority scenario (does the style's known profile serve it? at what tradeoff?) → run the accidental-complexity check (is any distributed mechanism present that no driver demands — e.g. a broker/queue/service split with no isolation/scale/deploy scenario behind it?) and the style-fallacy check (un-bounded sync chains, distributed-monolith coupling) → emit findings (`type:"decomposition"`, mismatched style vs driver = `major+`; un-driven complexity = `major`) + the risk register. Read-only — authors no artifact, edits no source.
- **design:** **Frame** (confirm the utility tree + the team-scale/independent-deploy/Conway facts; read-only) → **Explore** (≥2 genuinely divergent candidate styles as concrete one-page specs — e.g. *keep modular-monolith* vs *extract a service for context X* vs *event-driven for the deferred integration seam* — each with its deployment unit, consistency boundary, failure model, and team-ownership map; not the same boxes relabeled; read-only `architecture-explorer` ×N) → **Evaluate** (the skeptical `architecture-evaluator` builds the **decision matrix** — (H,*) scenarios weighted by priority × candidate styles — scores each against its response measure and tradeoff, finds the **sensitivity points** (the property that decides whether a scenario is met) and **tradeoff points** (a decision helping one attribute, hurting another — e.g. *service split improves fault isolation, worsens consistency + latency + ops*), order-swapped to kill position bias, penalizes accidental complexity, picks the winner and grafts the runners-up's seams where they don't break conceptual integrity; read-only) → **Document** (the ONE writer `solution-architect` authors the **one-way-door ADR** in `architecture/<slug>/adr/NNNN-architecture-style.md`, the decision matrix + sensitivity/tradeoff points in `architecture/<slug>/tradeoffs.md`, and the chosen style summary into `design-doc.md` + the C4-L2 container intent — reuses the project's existing stack/idioms, records the rejected styles and *why*) → **Verify** (traceability: every (H,*) scenario maps to how the chosen style serves it; the accidental-complexity gate; ADR-completeness incl. `One-way door? yes` + negative consequences; decision-matrix has ≥2 styles; the evaluator re-scores from the authored ADR/matrix, not the prose) → loop ≤10 or pass.

## Assertions & exit gate
- Every `(H,*)` quality-attribute scenario is traced to **how the chosen style serves it** (and at what tradeoff); no (H,*) scenario is left with no style-level answer.
- The decision matrix compares **≥2 styles** row-by-row against the prioritized scenarios; the winner's loss-cells (where a rejected style was better) are named, not hidden.
- **Accidental-complexity gate green:** no distributed/decoupled style is selected without a named (H,*) driver demanding it; the "you must be this tall" prerequisites (independent-deploy, Conway split, an isolation/scale scenario) are cited if a service split is chosen.
- The selection is a **complete MADR ADR** flagged **one-way door**, with context · ≥2 options · decision · positive AND negative consequences · driver refs to real `QAS-*` leaves.
- Any distributed candidate honors the 8 fallacies where they apply (no un-bounded sync chains, no distributed-monolith coupling).
- The chosen style preserves a deliberate **seam for the likely next change** (the near-term bounded contexts + any deferred integration boundary named in `project-architecture-config.md`) — recorded, not accidental.
- **Gate:** hard oracles green (≥2-style decision matrix traced to the utility tree · accidental-complexity check passed · one-way-door ADR complete with negative consequences · fallacies honored on any distributed candidate) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 — where right-sizedness and tradeoff-honesty are the load-bearing soft scores (the simpler style that meets the drivers scores higher).

## Output
Write `artifacts/architecture-style-selection/report.json` per `shared/report-format.md` — `drivers[]` (the (H,*) scenarios that drove the choice), `findings[]` per `shared/finding-schema.md` (`type:"decomposition"`; `oracle:"hard"` for an un-driven distributed style / a single-option "decision" / an untraced (H,*) scenario / a violated fallacy on a distributed candidate; `oracle:"soft"` for right-sizedness/tradeoff-honesty craft gaps), the `decisions[]` index pointing at the authored ADR, and the `verification` block (traceability of (H,*) scenarios → style, the accidental-complexity result, ADR-completeness, decision-matrix style-count, rubric score). The authored artifacts land in `architecture/<slug>/`: the one-way-door ADR in `adr/NNNN-architecture-style.md`, the decision matrix + sensitivity/tradeoff points in `tradeoffs.md`, the chosen-style narrative + C4-L2 intent in `design-doc.md` (per `project-architecture-config.md`). One-way-door flag and any deferred/open driver questions go in `notes[]`. Audit mode authors nothing — findings + risk register only.

## Guardrails
Per `shared/guardrails.md`: **propose & document the style + its tradeoffs — never write product code or apply infra**; the controller builds it, the operator deploys it. **Ground the choice in a driver** — no style without a (H,*) scenario it serves; a style no driver demands is accidental complexity, flag it, don't ship it. **Reuse the existing stack & boring tech first** (`project-architecture-config.md` locked rules) — a new datastore/broker/runtime needs its own driver-justified ADR with the operational-cost tradeoff. **Honor Conway** — don't draw service boundaries the small team can't own; favor the modular monolith over a service sprawl. **One writer** preserves conceptual integrity; the explorers/evaluator are read-only. **Record the tradeoff and the rejected styles**, never just the winner — the rejected alternatives are half the ADR's value. Cross-reference `decomposition-strategy.md` (the *within-style* module/service split + the distributed-monolith check that follows this choice), `../60-evaluation/tradeoff-analysis-cbam.md` (CBAM ROI when two styles tie on drivers), `shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md` (the canonical tradeoffs each candidate buys/pays), and `shared/adr-template.md` (the one-way-door ADR shape).
