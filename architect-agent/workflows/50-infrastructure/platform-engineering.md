# platform-engineering

**Group:** 50-infrastructure  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/infrastructure-architect.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** sonnet

## Purpose
Design the Internal Developer Platform (IDP) as a **product**, not a pile of tools — named capabilities, golden paths for the top developer journeys, self-service provisioning, and developer experience (DX) as the measured outcome. Answers the decision: *what is the thinnest set of platform capabilities that removes real developer friction on the top journeys (onboard → build → ship → operate), owned as a product with a roadmap and DX metrics, without adding cognitive load to the stream-aligned team it's meant to serve?*

## Inputs & preconditions
- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the project's stack, the deployment target and topology, the team topology (team size/composition, whether any dedicated platform headcount exists), and the constraint that a new platform tool/runtime needs a driver-justified ADR (boring-tech-preferred). Do **not** propose a full Backstage/Kubernetes-grade platform for a small team absent a concrete cognitive-load or scale driver.
- The **drivers / utility tree** (`../00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`): any modifiability, onboarding-speed, deployment-frequency, or operability scenario that motivates platform investment. If no TTFD/LTC/change-fail-rate target is stated in the source-of-truth docs, surface it as an **open driver question** — do not invent a number.
- The **team topology** (`../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md`): the ownership matrix and cognitive-load estimate per team — a platform team is only justified where a load signal exists; absent that, the platform capabilities are owned by the existing stream-aligned team as a backlog, not by a phantom platform team.
- The **delivery pipeline** (`cicd-architecture.md`): the existing/planned CI/CD shape — the platform's golden-path CI template must reuse it, not invent a parallel pipeline.
- The **IDP capability checklist** (`../shared/idp-capability-checklist.md`): the 12 capability areas, the maturity scale (None → Ad-hoc → Managed → Self-service → Optimized), and the DX-metric definitions (TTFD, LTC, shadow-IT rate) — this is the structured oracle both audit and design read against.
- Preconditions: the source-of-truth docs (BRD / PRD / understanding log) named in `project-architecture-config.md`, the repo's actual onboarding docs (`README.md`, setup scripts), current CI config, and any existing service/component inventory scanned first-hand — reuse what's there, don't re-derive.

## Oracle (source of truth)
The **CNCF "Platforms" Whitepaper** capability taxonomy + the **platform-as-a-product** model (Skelton/Pais, Team Topologies) + the DX metrics it prescribes. A platform is judged as a product against developer journeys and measured DX outcomes — never against how many tools it integrates.

**hard:**
- **Platform scoped as a product with named capabilities** — the proposal (or the audit of an existing platform) covers each of the 12 capability areas in `idp-capability-checklist.md` with a stated current-state maturity level (0–4) and an explicit gap vs the drivers. A capability area silently skipped (not scored, not flagged out-of-scope-with-reason) is a hard finding — an unscoped platform is not a product, it's a wishlist. *Structural check:* the capability-coverage table has one row per §1–12 with a maturity score and a gap statement; any missing row blocks.
- **Golden paths defined for the top developer journeys** — at minimum: (a) new-service scaffolding → CI/CD-wired repo, (b) code change → deployed-to-lowest-env artifact, (c) an alert firing → a runbook. Each golden path is named, has an owner, and its absence for a journey the drivers call out as high-value is a hard finding. *Structural check:* per `idp-capability-checklist.md` §1–2, the day-one guide and the scaffolding template exist (or are the explicit design deliverable) and are traceable to a driver.
- **Self-service provisioning, not ticket-mediated** — the target maturity for onboarding, CI/CD, and secrets (§1, §2, §6) is **Level 3 (Self-service)**: a developer executes the capability without an ops ticket. A capability proposed to stay at Level 1/2 (ad-hoc/managed) with no stated reason is a hard finding — ticket-mediated provisioning is the opposite of a golden path.
- **DX metrics named and measured** — time-to-first-deploy (TTFD), lead time for change (LTC), and change-fail rate (the DORA triad, per `idp-capability-checklist.md`) each have a stated target (traced to a driver or explicitly surfaced as an open question) and a named measurement point (where in the pipeline/tooling the metric is captured). A platform proposal with no DX metric — "the platform will help developers" with no number — fails the grounding rule (`quality-attribute-rubric.md`). *Structural check:* TTFD ≤ 1 day target (or the project-specific target) is stated; if unmet or unmeasured, that is the primary finding, ranked above any capability-area gap.
- **Net reduction in stream-team cognitive load** — the proposal must show, for each capability it adds, which cognitive-load category it removes from the stream-aligned team (intrinsic/extraneous/germane, per `../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md`) — never a net *increase* in load with no offsetting removal. A platform capability that requires the stream team to learn and operate a new tool with no automation behind it (e.g., "now maintain your own Helm charts") without removing an equivalent burden is a hard finding — it fails the "platform reduces load" test, regardless of how capable the tool is.
- **ADR-complete for each platform-technology decision** — build-vs-buy (self-hosted Backstage vs a managed portal vs no catalog yet), the golden-path CI template choice, and the secrets-management approach are each an ADR with context · ≥2 options · decision · positive **and** negative consequences (`../shared/adr-template.md`). A platform tool introduced without a recorded alternative is résumé-driven, not driver-driven.

**soft:**
- **Thinnest viable platform** — the platform is scaled to the **Thinnest Viable Platform** heuristic (Team Topologies): the minimum capability set that removes *real, evidenced* developer friction for this team size, not the maximum a whitepaper lists. No capability is added "because CNCF says so" without a friction signal (a support-ticket pattern, a shadow-IT instance, an onboarding-time complaint) tracing to it.
- **Adoption by attraction, not mandate** — a golden path wins because it's measurably easier than the alternative (fewer steps, faster TTFD), not because a policy forces its use. A design that relies on a mandate ("all services must use the platform") with no attraction argument (why would a developer *choose* this path?) is a soft finding — mandated platforms accrue shadow-IT.
- **Right-sized platform-team question** — for a small team, "the platform team" is almost certainly the same people wearing a platform hat part-time, not a dedicated org; the proposal should say so explicitly rather than implying a platform org exists.

## Standards & techniques
- **Platform engineering** (Humanitec/CNCF-adjacent industry practice, 2022–2026) — the discipline of building and operating an IDP as an internal product; the platform engineer's customer is the application developer, and platform work is prioritized by developer pain, not ops convenience.
- **CNCF "Platforms" Whitepaper (2023) capability taxonomy** — the reference model this flow's hard oracle is built on (`../shared/idp-capability-checklist.md` §1–12: onboarding/golden-paths, CI/CD, infra orchestration, environment management, observability, secrets/identity, service catalog, cost/FinOps, security/policy guardrails, API/integration platform, data platform, support/docs). Use it to check nothing material is silently missing, the same role ISO/IEC 25010 plays for quality attributes.
- **Internal Developer Platform (IDP)** — the sum of the tooling + workflows an organization builds to let developers self-serve the golden paths; distinct from "DevOps tooling" in that it's productized (a roadmap, an owner, a feedback loop), not a toolbox.
- **Backstage / service catalog** (Spotify OSS, CNCF-incubated) — the reference implementation of the service-catalog capability (`idp-capability-checklist.md` §7): every service has an owner, an SLO, a runbook, discoverable via one place. Cite as the pattern even where the concrete tool chosen is lighter-weight (a markdown catalog, a Port/OpsLevel SaaS) for a small team.
- **Golden paths / paved roads** (Spotify engineering culture term, popularized via platform-engineering practice) — a curated, pre-validated, self-service route through a capability area that delivers the right outcome for ~80% of teams without needing infrastructure knowledge. The golden path is optional but so much easier it's rarely skipped — this is the "attraction not mandate" soft oracle.
- **Platform-as-a-product + DX metrics** — treat the internal developer population as customers; the platform has a backlog prioritized by developer pain (support tickets, survey NPS, shadow-IT rate), not by what's technically interesting. DX metrics (TTFD, LTC, shadow-IT rate — `idp-capability-checklist.md`) are the platform's own quality-attribute scenarios: they get a response measure like any other driver.
- **Thinnest Viable Platform (TVP)** (Team Topologies, Skelton & Pais) — start with the smallest platform that unblocks the stream-aligned team's biggest friction point; grow capability incrementally as load signals accumulate, never front-load the full CNCF taxonomy on day one. Pair with the **cognitive-load** lens from `../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md`: a platform capability exists to *remove* extraneous load from the stream team, and if it doesn't reduce load somewhere, it's not earning its keep.

- **Maturity scale as the current-state oracle** — score each capability area None/Ad-hoc/Managed/Self-service/Optimized (`idp-capability-checklist.md`); this is how "audit" and "design" share the same instrument — an audit measures where the org sits today, a design proposes the path to Level 3+ on the capabilities the drivers demand.

## Step sequence
- **audit:** Frame the drivers (any stated onboarding-time, deployment-frequency, or developer-friction driver; surface unstated DX targets as open questions) → walk each of the 12 capability areas in `idp-capability-checklist.md` against the actual repo/tooling (onboarding docs, CI config, secrets handling, service inventory, observability setup) and assign a current maturity level + a gap statement per area → compute or estimate the DX metrics where measurable (TTFD from a fresh-clone timing, LTC from commit-to-deploy history if available) and flag any missing measurement as a gap, not a pass → check for shadow-IT signals (bespoke per-developer scripts standing in for a missing capability) → emit findings + a risk register grouped by `type` then severity (read-only, authors no artifact, edits no source, never proposes a platform-team org change without a load signal).
- **design:** **Frame** (confirm the utility tree — onboarding-speed, deployment-frequency, operability scenarios; read `../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md` for the current ownership/cognitive-load picture and `cicd-architecture.md` for the existing pipeline shape; surface unknowns as open questions) → **Explore** (`architecture-explorer` ×N produces divergent platform scopes as concrete specs — e.g. *(a)* Thinnest Viable Platform: a golden-path repo template + a single golden-path CI workflow + a markdown-based lightweight service catalog, owned part-time by the existing team, targeting Level 3 on §1/§2/§6 only; *(b)* a broader platform: adds a hosted Backstage instance for the service catalog (§7) and a dedicated cost dashboard (§8), justified only if a load/scale signal from the audit supports it; each option names its capability-area coverage, target maturity per area, DX-metric targets, and the cognitive-load delta for the stream team; read-only) → **Evaluate** (`architecture-evaluator` scores each option against the utility tree + the hard oracles via ATAM; locates the **sensitivity point** — TTFD is highly sensitive to whether scaffolding auto-wires CI/observability/secrets vs requiring manual post-scaffold steps — and the **tradeoff point** — a hosted service catalog buys discoverability but costs a new operational dependency and onboarding overhead for a small team; runs CBAM so a heavier platform is chosen only where the friction signal and the load reduction justify the build/run cost over the TVP baseline; order-swapped to kill bias; penalizes any option that adds tooling with no matching friction evidence) → **Document** (the ONE writer — `solution-architect` — authors the capability-coverage table (all 12 areas, current + target maturity, gap, golden-path owner), the golden-path specs for the top journeys, the DX-metric targets + measurement points, and **one ADR per platform-technology decision** (service-catalog build-vs-buy, golden-path CI template choice, secrets-management approach) into `architecture/<slug>/`; reuses the existing CI/CD and team-topology artifacts rather than re-deriving them; preserves conceptual integrity with the rest of the infrastructure package) → **Verify** (the capability-coverage completeness check — all 12 areas scored, no silent gaps; the DX-metric traceability — each metric has a target and a measurement point; the cognitive-load-net-reduction check against `team-topology-decomposition.md`'s ownership matrix; diagram-compile on the golden-path flow diagram; the evaluator re-scores from the authored artifacts, not the prose) → loop ≤ 10 or pass.

## Assertions & exit gate
- Every one of the 12 `idp-capability-checklist.md` capability areas has a stated current-state maturity level (0–4) and a gap statement — no area silently skipped.
- At least the onboarding, CI/CD, and secrets capability areas (§1, §2, §6) target **Level 3 (Self-service)** or the shortfall is an explicit, driver-traced open question — not a silent Level 1/2 acceptance.
- Golden paths are named for the top developer journeys (scaffold-to-CI-wired-repo, commit-to-deployed-artifact, alert-to-runbook) with an owner each.
- DX metrics (TTFD, LTC, change-fail rate / shadow-IT rate) each have a stated target (traced to a driver, or listed as an open driver question) and a named measurement point.
- Every capability added shows its **cognitive-load offset** for the stream-aligned team — net load reduction, not addition, traced against `../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md`'s ownership/load estimates.
- Every platform-technology choice (service catalog, golden-path CI template, secrets store) is a complete ADR — context · ≥2 options · decision · positive + negative consequences.
- The platform is sized to the **Thinnest Viable Platform** for this team — any capability with no traced friction signal is flagged as an over-engineering risk, not shipped silently.
- **Gate:** hard oracles green (capability-coverage complete · golden paths named · self-service target on §1/§2/§6 · DX metrics traced+measured · net cognitive-load reduction · ADRs complete) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 (thinnest-viable-platform discipline · adoption-by-attraction · right-sized platform-team framing).

## Output
Write `artifacts/platform-engineering/report.json` per `../shared/report-format.md` — findings/risks per `../shared/finding-schema.md` (`type:"operability"` / `"evolvability"` / `"cost"` / `"risk"` as appropriate; `oracle:"hard"` for a skipped capability area, a missing golden path for a top journey, a capability stuck at ticket-mediated provisioning with no reason, an untargeted/unmeasured DX metric, a net cognitive-load increase with no offset, or an incomplete platform-technology ADR; `oracle:"soft"` for a capability added with no friction signal, a mandate-based rather than attraction-based golden path, or an implied dedicated platform org where none is staffed). Each finding carries its `driver_ref` (the onboarding/deployment-frequency/operability `QAS-*` or the team-topology cognitive-load constraint it protects), the traced structural fact in `evidence` (e.g. "no scaffolding template exists — new-service setup is a 12-step manual doc, TTFD unmeasured but anecdotally > 1 day"), and a `tradeoff` + `alternatives_rejected` (e.g. "hosted Backstage buys a discoverable service catalog at the cost of a new operational dependency for a small team; a markdown catalog in the repo rejected only if scale grows past ~5 services"). The verification block includes the capability-coverage table (12 areas × maturity × gap), the DX-metric traceability table (metric → target → measurement point), and the cognitive-load-offset table (capability → team → load category removed). Authored artifacts (the capability-coverage table, golden-path specs, the platform-technology ADRs, the golden-path flow diagram) land in `architecture/<slug>/` per `project-architecture-config.md`. Open driver questions (unstated TTFD/LTC targets, unconfirmed platform-team staffing) are listed explicitly for the controller — never silently assumed.

## Guardrails
Per `../shared/guardrails.md`: **propose & document — never write product code and never apply infra** (no scaffolding tool executed, no CI workflow committed, no catalog instance stood up — author the *platform design and the ADRs*; the controller builds the golden-path tooling, the operator provisions any hosted service). **Ground every capability in a driver or a friction signal** — no capability area targets Level 3+ without a driver or an evidenced developer-pain signal; a capability added "because the whitepaper lists it" is accidental complexity, flag it, don't ship it. **Reuse known patterns before inventing** — golden paths, service catalogs, and the CNCF taxonomy are the established reference; a bespoke platform mechanism needs a driver-justified ADR showing why the standard pattern doesn't fit. **One writer** authors the platform design so it has conceptual integrity with the rest of the infrastructure package; the read-only `architecture-evaluator` scores it and never edits it. **Record the tradeoff and the rejected alternatives** on every platform-technology decision — what the chosen scope costs in operational surface area vs what it buys in DX. Cross-reference `../shared/idp-capability-checklist.md` (the capability-coverage oracle this flow gates on), `../10-styles-decomposition/team-topology-decomposition.md` (the ownership matrix and cognitive-load estimates a platform must reduce, not add to), and `cicd-architecture.md` (the golden-path CI template must reuse the delivery pipeline design, not fork it) before authoring any capability proposal — these are not optional reads; a platform designed without its team-topology and pipeline context is a floating wishlist, not a product.
