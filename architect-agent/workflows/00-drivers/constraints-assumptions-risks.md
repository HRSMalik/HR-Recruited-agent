# constraints-assumptions-risks

**Group:** 00-drivers  ·  **Runs as:** subagent: ../.claude/agents/architecture-orchestrator.md  ·  **Mode:** audit + design  ·  **Default model:** sonnet

## Purpose

Establish the **boundary conditions every later decision must respect**: (1) the non-negotiable constraints (technical, business, and regulatory ceilings that cannot be designed around), (2) the assumptions the design rests on (each one unvalidated is a latent risk), and (3) the initial **RAID register** (Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies) with likelihood × impact scores and dispositions. This flow produces the outer envelope the utility tree (`workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`) and every downstream decision must stay within; decisions that violate a constraint block regardless of their quality-attribute score.

## Inputs & preconditions

- From `project-architecture-config.md`: the hard constraints block (stack lock, deployment topology, phase scope, team topology/Conway constraints, compliance regime), the architectural-drivers seed, the system-under-design description, and the artifact root (`architecture/<slug>/`).
- The functional scope and the prioritized quality-attribute scenarios (the utility tree from `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md`), if already authored — constraints and the utility tree are co-dependent and may be drafted in parallel; reconcile any conflicts before advancing.
- Source documents: the source-of-truth docs (BRD / PRD / understanding log, and any ticket-map or backlog index) named in `project-architecture-config.md`. Read these before deriving constraints — never invent a constraint or assumption the source docs don't support; if unclear, surface it as an open question.
- Preconditions: source docs readable; `project-architecture-config.md` present (or auto-bootstrapped by the orchestrator before this flow runs); the scope (system / subsystem / greenfield) is stated. If scope is undefined, this flow returns `status:error` — it cannot operate without a boundary.

## Oracle (source of truth)

The constraints taxonomy applied to the stated project constraints, the assumptions log, and the RAID register — all grounded in the source docs and the `project-architecture-config.md`.

- **hard:**
  - Every constraint is recorded as **non-negotiable with its source traced** (the doc, section, or architectural-config entry that mandates it). A constraint with no source is a preference, not a constraint — either trace it or drop it.
  - Every assumption is logged with a **validation status** (`validated | unvalidated | refuted`) and, for unvalidated ones, a validation method and owner. An unvalidated assumption is treated as a latent risk and appears in the risk register.
  - Every risk entry carries: a **likelihood × impact** pair (each H / M / L with a rationale), a **disposition** (accept / mitigate / monitor — proposed, final call belongs to the controller), and the **scenario it threatens** (a QAS-* id from the utility tree, or a named constraint if the utility tree is not yet final). A risk with no disposition and no threatened scenario is incomplete and blocks.
  - The **constraints taxonomy** is applied in full — all three classes must be covered or explicitly declared empty: (a) **technical constraints** (the project's stack lock, runtime/language/datastore, deployment topology, team toolchain, existing API contracts that cannot break — per `project-architecture-config.md`), (b) **business constraints** (budget ceiling, timeline/phase gates, build-vs-buy mandates, contractual/SLA commitments), (c) **regulatory / compliance constraints** (data residency, PII handling obligations, audit-log requirements, RBAC mandates, the applicable regime — e.g. healthcare / financial / general data-protection, per `project-architecture-config.md`). A class left blank without explicit "none identified" is a gap.
  - Dependencies in the RAID register name the **external party or team, the deliverable, and the date/milestone they are needed by**; a dependency with no owner is flagged as a risk.

- **soft:**
  - Assumptions are **surfaced, not buried** — the design document never relies on an implicit assumption the reader has to infer; every load-bearing assumption appears in the assumptions log.
  - Constraints are **real boundaries, not disguised preferences** — the taxonomy distinguishes "this cannot change (constraint)" from "we'd prefer not to change this (preference)"; inflating preferences into constraints bloats the constraint set and disguises design options.
  - The risk register is **honest about one-way-door risks** — risks that, if materialized, eliminate a design option or require a rewrite are flagged `one_way_door: true` and receive higher scrutiny (not buried at `minor`).
  - The register is **actionable**: each entry names who acts on it and by when. A risk register that is only a list of worries with no dispositions fails the craft bar.

## Standards & techniques

- **Constraints taxonomy** (three-class partition): technical / business / regulatory. Applied in that order; each class is explicitly confirmed or declared empty. Source every entry (doc + section or config entry) before recording it.
- **Assumptions log** (unvalidated-assumption-as-latent-risk principle): an assumption is a statement believed true but not yet verified. The log tracks: statement, category (technical / organizational / market / regulatory), validation status, validation method, owner. Every unvalidated assumption propagates to the risk register as a risk with `type:"risk"` and `likelihood` informed by how consequential the assumption being wrong would be.
- **RAID** (Risks / Assumptions / Issues / Dependencies): the canonical project-management risk artifact, applied here as an architecture-level instrument. Risks are architectural threats; Assumptions belong to the assumptions log (cross-referenced); Issues are constraints or gaps that are *already* active (known now, not future); Dependencies name external pre-conditions the architecture relies on but does not own.
- **Risk = likelihood × impact**: each axis H / M / L with a one-line rationale. Disposition: accept (log, no action), mitigate (an ADR or design change reduces it), monitor (track; escalate if crosses a threshold). Severity on the finding object maps to risk level: (H, H) → `critical`, (H, M) or (M, H) → `major`, (M, M) → `minor`, (L, *) or (*, L) → `cosmetic` unless it threatens a one-way-door decision.
- **One-way-door flag**: borrowed from the ATAM / reversibility dimension of `shared/quality-attribute-rubric.md` and `shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`. Any constraint or risk that, if wrong, requires dismantling a hard-to-reverse decision (the datastore, the auth model, the prod-deploy topology, a public API contract) is flagged `one_way_door: true`. These get the deepest validation effort and are escalated to the controller before a design is authored around them.
- **ATAM sensitivity point linkage**: a constraint or assumption that, if it changed, would strongly determine whether a quality-attribute scenario is met is noted as a **sensitivity point** (per `shared/evaluation-method.md`). These feed directly into the ATAM evaluation run in `workflows/60-evaluation/risk-storming-premortem.md`.
- **Fallacies crosscheck** (`shared/fallacies-and-tradeoffs.md`): scan the assumptions log for any implicit belief in the 8 fallacies (the network is reliable, latency is zero, bandwidth is infinite, the network is secure, topology doesn't change, there is one administrator, transport is free, the network is homogeneous). Each one found in an assumption is immediately flagged `unvalidated` and a risk entry is created under the fallacy that is being assumed away.
- **MADR-shaped constraint records**: constraints that represent genuine architectural forks (e.g. "stack lock: the project's chosen backend framework + primary datastore, per `project-architecture-config.md` — a new datastore needs a driver-justified ADR") are recorded as a constraint statement plus a forward pointer to the ADR that would be required to override them. This preserves the option to revisit without dismantling the constraint silently.

## Step sequence

- **audit:** Read `project-architecture-config.md` → read the source-of-truth docs it names (BRD / PRD / understanding log) → apply the three-class constraints taxonomy (technical / business / regulatory) to what is currently stated; flag any class that is blank without explicit confirmation → scan assumptions embedded in the existing architecture description (if one exists) and restate them explicitly; flag each as validated / unvalidated → check for fallacy-assuming beliefs in the existing assumption set → check that every existing risk entry has likelihood × impact + disposition + threatened scenario → emit findings (read-only, no artifact authored); group by type then severity (critical → cosmetic); produce a risk register of `type:"risk"` findings with likelihood × impact and dispositions proposed → **emit the risk-driven effort gate** (see §Risk-Driven Effort Gate below): derive the highest likelihood×impact band and flag all `one_way_door:true` items; record the recommended evaluation-effort tier; this gate output is the entry condition `shared/evaluation-method.md` uses to select the evaluation method.
- **design:** Frame (read `project-architecture-config.md` + source docs; confirm the scope and the three-class taxonomy; read-only) → Explore (draft the full constraints set, the assumptions log, and the RAID register; explicitly ask: "what must be true for this design to work?" and "what could make it fail?"; surface at least one sensitivity point per high-priority QAS scenario; read-only) → Evaluate (skeptical evaluator cross-checks: every constraint is sourced, every assumption has a validation status, every risk has likelihood × impact + disposition + threatened QAS, fallacies crosscheck clean, one-way-door risks flagged; order-swapped; read-only) → **Emit risk-driven effort gate** (derive highest severity band + one-way-door flag from the RAID register; record the recommended evaluation-effort tier — single ADR / Lightweight-ATAM / full ATAM; this becomes the evaluation-method entry condition per `shared/risk-register.md` §4 and `shared/evaluation-method.md` § "How much evaluation?"; the gate decision must be recorded before advancing past Explore) → Document (one writer authors `architecture/<slug>/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` — the three-class constraint table, the assumptions log with validation status, the RAID register typed per `shared/risk-register.md`; write the fitness-function spec to `architecture/<slug>/fitness-functions.md` — the "no new unconstrained technology" and "every assumption validated before design-freeze" checks) → Verify (fitness functions: constraint-source completeness check; assumption-log coverage vs design doc; risk disposition completeness; fallacies crosscheck; risk-driven effort gate recorded and traceable; evaluator re-scores from the artifact; loop ≤ 8 or pass).

## Risk-Driven Effort Gate

This flow is the **sole producer** of the structured risk register that gates evaluation-method selection. Both audit and design modes must emit it before any downstream evaluation flow (`workflows/60-evaluation/`) proceeds.

**What the gate produces:**

1. **Structured risk register** — every RAID risk entry serialised per the `shared/risk-register.md` schema (fields: `id`, `description`, `likelihood`, `impact`, `severity`, `disposition`, `qas_ref`, `one_way_door`, `open`, `mitigation_note`, `finding_ref`). The in-progress instance lives at `architecture/<slug>/risk-register.json`; the human-readable summary is the RAID Register table in `architecture/<slug>/constraints-assumptions-risks.md`.

2. **Effort-tier recommendation** — derived from the register by applying `shared/risk-register.md` §4 (Steps 1–4). The recommendation is one of:
   - `single-adr` — all open risks Low or Medium, no one-way doors.
   - `lightweight-atam` — any High open risk, or any one-way-door item at any severity.
   - `full-atam` — any Critical open risk, or one-way-door + High; CBAM appended when two compliant options exist.
   - `tara` — the architecture is already implemented; metric + fitness-function scan is the appropriate path regardless of register state.

3. **Gate record** — the four-line trace per `shared/risk-register.md` §4 Step 4:
   ```
   Risk-gate: highest open severity = <band>; one-way-door = <true|false>
   → Selected method: <method name from evaluation-method.md>
   → Justification: <cite the register items that drove the selection>
   ```
   This trace is included in `report.json` under `verification.risk_gate` and cross-referenced in `architecture/<slug>/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` at the foot of the RAID Register section.

**Entry condition for `shared/evaluation-method.md`:** `evaluation-method.md` § "How much evaluation?" reads the risk register from `architecture/<slug>/risk-register.json` (or from this flow's `report.json` → `risk_register[]` array in audit mode). It must not select a method without first checking this gate. A method selection that skips the gate is a hard finding (`oracle:"hard"`, `severity:"major"`).

**Cross-references:** `shared/risk-register.md` (schema + gate algorithm) · `shared/evaluation-method.md` (method menu + entry conditions) · `workflows/60-evaluation/risk-storming-premortem.md` (downstream consumer of the register's sensitivity points).

## Assertions & exit gate

- **Constraint completeness:** all three constraint classes (technical / business / regulatory) are present; each entry cites its source (doc + section or `project-architecture-config.md` entry); no class is silently empty.
- **Assumption traceability:** every assumption has a validation status; every `unvalidated` assumption appears in the risk register; no assumption is implicit in a design doc without an explicit entry in the log.
- **Risk register completeness:** every risk entry carries likelihood (H/M/L) + impact (H/M/L) + rationale + disposition (accept/mitigate/monitor) + the QAS id or constraint it threatens; no entry is missing any of these fields.
- **One-way-door coverage:** every (H,H) risk and every risk tied to an irreversible decision is flagged `one_way_door: true`; controller notified before a design is authored over it.
- **Fallacies crosscheck clean:** no assumption in the log silently restates one of the 8 fallacies without a corresponding risk entry and a design account for the fallacy's reality.
- **ATAM sensitivity points linked:** every constraint or unvalidated assumption that is a sensitivity point for a high-priority QAS is explicitly cross-referenced to the scenario id, ready for `workflows/60-evaluation/risk-storming-premortem.md` to consume.
- **Dependency completeness:** every RAID dependency names an external party/team, a specific deliverable, and a date or milestone; undated or ownerless dependencies are flagged as risks.
- **Risk register schema conformance:** every risk entry in the RAID register is serialised per `shared/risk-register.md` (all required fields present: `id`, `description`, `likelihood`, `impact`, `severity` derived from matrix, `disposition`, `qas_ref`, `one_way_door`, `open`, `mitigation_note` when `disposition ≠ accept`). A register entry missing any required field blocks the gate.
- **Effort-tier recommendation present:** the risk-driven effort gate (§Risk-Driven Effort Gate) has been evaluated and the four-line gate record is present in `report.json` → `verification.risk_gate` and in `architecture/<slug>/constraints-assumptions-risks.md`. An evaluation-method selection by any downstream flow without this gate record is a hard finding.

**Gate:** hard oracles green (every constraint sourced, every assumption logged with validation status, every risk entry complete with likelihood × impact + disposition + threatened scenario per `shared/risk-register.md`, fallacies crosscheck clean, one-way-door risks flagged, effort-tier recommendation recorded with gate trace) AND (design) rubric mean ≥ 0.8 (assumptions surfaced not buried, constraints real not inflated, risk register honest and actionable).

## Output

- Write `artifacts/constraints-assumptions-risks/report.json` per `shared/report-format.md` — findings per `finding-schema.md` (`type:"driver"` for an untraced constraint or a missing class, `type:"risk"` for every RAID risk entry with likelihood × impact; `oracle:"hard"` for missing validation status on an assumption, a risk with no disposition, a constraint with no source, or a fallacy assumed away without an account; `oracle:"soft"` for a buried assumption or a preference inflated into a constraint). In **design mode** `report.json` must carry the full **verification block** (`shared/report-format.md` § verification): `artifacts[]` listing the authored files under `architecture/<slug>/`; `traceability` coverage (constraint rows sourced, assumptions logged, risk entries complete vs total); `fitness_functions[]` results for the two checks defined in this flow (constraint-source check + assumption-validation-gate); `hard_oracles{}` keyed to the nine exit-gate checks (constraint completeness, assumption traceability, risk register completeness, one-way-door coverage, fallacies crosscheck, ATAM sensitivity points linked, dependency completeness, risk register schema conformance, effort-tier recommendation present); and `rubric_score` from the `architecture-evaluator`'s craft pass. A design-mode report without this block is `status: fail`.
- In **design mode** the one writer (`solution-architect`) authors the constraint/assumption/risk artifact into `architecture/<slug>/constraints-assumptions-risks.md` with three sections: (1) Constraint Inventory — a three-class table (technical / business / regulatory), each row: constraint statement | source | one-way-door flag | override ADR required?; (2) Assumptions Log — each row: assumption statement | category | validation status | validation method | owner | linked risk id if unvalidated; (3) RAID Register — each risk row serialised per `shared/risk-register.md` §1 (id `RISK-<DOMAIN>-NNN` | statement | likelihood × impact | severity (matrix-derived) | disposition | threatened QAS or constraint | owner | one-way-door flag | open/closed), followed at the foot of the section by the four-line **risk-gate trace** (per §Risk-Driven Effort Gate above) recording the highest open severity, the one-way-door flag, the selected evaluation-effort tier, and its justification. The same register is serialised machine-readably to `architecture/<slug>/risk-register.json` per `shared/risk-register.md` §1 — this is the file `shared/evaluation-method.md` § "How much evaluation?" reads as its entry condition; the Markdown table is the human-readable mirror, not a separate source of truth.
- Fitness-function spec (design mode only): write to `architecture/<slug>/fitness-functions.md` (or append if the file exists) the two architectural-characteristic checks this flow mandates: (a) **constraint-source check** — every row in the constraint table must cite a traceable source; a row without one fails; (b) **assumption-validation-gate** — before the architecture is marked ready for implementation (design-freeze milestone), every `unvalidated` assumption in the log must be resolved to `validated` or `refuted` and the risk register updated accordingly.
- Findings reference the finding-schema `type:"risk"` object for all RAID risk entries; the risk register in `report.json` is the machine-readable RAID. Constraint-class gaps are `type:"driver"`, `severity:"major"` (a missing class can hide a blocking constraint). A constraint with a sourced entry that turns out to be a preference (not a real ceiling) is `type:"driver"`, `severity:"minor"`, `oracle:"soft"` — no driver blocker, craft gap only.
- Cross-references: the authored artifact links forward to `workflows/60-evaluation/risk-storming-premortem.md` for the collaborative risk-storming step, and back to `workflows/00-drivers/quality-attribute-scenarios.md` for the QAS ids the risks threaten.

## Guardrails

Per `shared/guardrails.md`: propose & document — never write product code; ground every constraint in a stated source, every risk in a driver or a named scenario. Audit mode is read-only (no artifact authored, no source edited). Design mode: exactly one writer authors the constraint/assumption/risk artifact — proposer and evaluator are read-only. The evaluator never grades the artifact it co-drafted; it is a separate skeptical pass that independently re-checks each hard oracle before scoring craft. Never invent a number (likelihood, impact, count) without a basis in the source docs or the project config — if a value is unknown, surface it as an open question. Constraint records that name a one-way-door decision are escalated to the controller before any downstream design flow proceeds past the Explore step; do not paper over a one-way-door risk to keep the design loop moving. The risk dispositions (accept/mitigate/monitor) proposed here are provisional — the controller makes the final call.
